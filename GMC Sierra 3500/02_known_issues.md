# GMC Sierra 3500 HD — Known Issues by Year
*6.6L Duramax LML (2011–2016) / L5P (2017–Present) · Allison · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> ⚠️ **CRITICAL CORRECTION FROM DEEP DIVE — READ THIS FIRST:**
> The 6.6L Duramax LML (2011–2016) uses the **Bosch CP4.2** injection pump — NOT the CP3.
> The CP3 applies only to the L5P (2017+) and all pre-2011 Duramax engines (LB7, LLY, LBZ, LMM).
> If you are looking at a used Sierra from 2011–2016, **it carries the same CP4 risk as the Ford F-350.**
> The "safe CP3" advantage kicks in at 2017 and later only.

---

> **Platform note:** The Sierra 3500 HD and Silverado 3500 HD share the same frame, engine, transmission, axles, and drivetrain. All mechanical known issues are identical. This document notes Sierra-specific recalls, trim differences, and the AT4-specific hardware items that don't apply to the Silverado.

## 2011–2014 — LML Duramax, First Years

### 🔴 CP4.2 Injection Pump *(2011–2016 LML)*
- Same CP4.2 failure risk as the Chevy Silverado 3500 — identical fuel system
- Metal shrapnel failure mode; $8,000–$20,000 remediation cost
- CP3 conversion kits available from multiple vendors ($1,200–$2,500 installed)
- **Strongly recommend CP3 conversion on any LML Sierra purchase**

### 🟠 EGR Cooler Failure *(2011–2014)*
- EGR cooler failures are more frequent on early LML production
- White smoke, coolant loss, elevated temps
- **Fix:** EGR cooler replacement ($400–$900 parts + labor)

### 🟠 DEF Pump & Doser Failure *(2013–2014)*
- Same as Silverado: early DEF system (2013 was LML's first SCR year) had pump and dosing injector failures
- **Fix:** DEF pump ($400–$800), DEF doser ($200–$500)

### 🟠 NOx Sensor Failure *(2013–2016)*
- Triggers reduced power/derate events
- **Fix:** Sensor replacement ($200–$400)

### 🟡 Dual Fuel Tank Overflow *(2011–2015 Sierra HD, if dual-tank equipped)*
- NHTSA recall: on Sierra HD models with the optional dual fuel tank, front tank overflow could allow fuel to contact the driveshaft
- Fire risk in extreme scenarios
- **NHTSA Recall 14V-592 (and related):** Check VIN for completion status
- Not all Sierras have dual fuel tanks; verify before purchasing

### 🟡 Allison 6-Speed Fluid Maintenance *(all Allison years)*
- Allison TranSynd fluid is mandatory — not optional
- Heavy tow use: change every 25,000 miles
- Normal use: every 50,000 miles

---

## 2015–2016 — LML Duramax, Late Production

### 🔴 CP4.2 *(continues through 2016)*
- Still CP4.2 in 2015–2016 LML trucks
- By 2025–2026, these trucks are 9–11 years old; CP4 failures increasingly common in enthusiast community at this mileage
- **Recommendation:** Price in CP3 conversion or require it done before purchase

### 🟠 DEF System Aging *(2015–2016)*
- DEF heater failures, DEF tank assembly replacements, DEF pump failures increasing as trucks age
- More refined than 2013–2014 design but components don't last forever
- **Fix:** Component replacement as needed; DEF tank assembly is most expensive ($800–$1,500 installed)

---

## 2017 — L5P Duramax Debut ✅ CP3 Returns

Same transition as the Silverado 3500 — 2017 is the inflection point.

### ✅ CP3 — Safe from Here Forward
- L5P uses Bosch CP3
- The return of CP3 is the most important single improvement in the 2017 refresh

### 🟠 Water Pump Failure *(2017–2018, worst years)*
- L5P water pump failures concentrated in first two model years
- TSBs released; many repaired under warranty for original owners
- **Fix:** Water pump replacement ($400–$900). Check service records on any used 2017–2018.

### 🟠 Coolant Air Pocket *(early 2017 production)*
- Factory assembly line left some trucks with air in the cooling system
- **Fix:** Cooling system bleed procedure — quick and cheap

### 🟡 Fuel Injector Imbalance Codes *(2017–present)*
- L5P injector balance codes (P0263–P0278 range) are a maintenance indicator, not an emergency
- **Fix:** Monitor; replace drifting injectors as needed (~$400–$600 each installed)

---

## 2018–2019 — L5P, 3rd Gen Body (Sierra 3500)

### 🟡 Water Pump *(improving, less common than 2017)*
- 2018 still saw some; 2019 largely clean

### 🟡 DPF Clogging *(short-trip use)*
- L5P DPF requires periodic highway regen cycles
- Trucks used for short trips or heavy idle time accumulate soot faster
- **Fix:** Highway drive or forced regen at dealer

### 🟡 DEF Tank Heater *(cold climates)*
- Still present as a cold-weather issue
- **Fix:** Heater replacement ($300–$600)

---

## 2020–2022 — 4th Gen Sierra 3500 (New Body + AT4 Available)

The AT4 package launched with the 4th generation (2020). Any AT4 Sierra 3500 is from 2020 or later — there is no AT4 on any earlier Sierra 3500.

### ✅ Same L5P + CP3
- No change to fuel system; CP3 continues

### 🟠 Allison 10-Speed Shift Programming *(2020–2021)*
- The new 10L1000 launched with shift quality issues (harsh in some scenarios, hunting under moderate load)
- GM released multiple TCM software updates
- **Recommendation:** Confirm all Allison TCM software updates are current before buying a 2020–2021
- By 2022+, largely resolved

### 🟡 Block Heater Cord — Electrical Short *(2017–2019 Sierra HD Recall, some carried to 2020)*
- NHTSA recall on Sierra HD block heater cords for electrical short circuit and fire risk
- Primarily 2017–2019 production but verify VIN for 2020 units as well
- **Recall:** Check VIN at nhtsa.gov — remedy is block heater cord replacement (dealer fix, free)

### 🟡 DEF Gauge Software Glitch *(2020–2021)*
- Some 2020–2021 Sierra 3500s displayed inaccurate DEF level readings due to a software bug
- Could trigger unnecessary derate warnings when DEF was actually adequate
- **Fix:** ECU software update at dealer — typically addressed under warranty or as a no-charge TSB

### 🟡 AT4-Specific: DSSV Damper Wear *(2020+ AT4)*
- Multimatic DSSV dampers are excellent when new but are more expensive to replace than standard shocks
- At high mileage (150K+), replacement cost is notably higher than standard shocks
- **Fix:** Multimatic DSSV damper replacement (~$400–$600 each vs. ~$100–$200 for standard). Plan for it; budget accordingly.

---

## 2022 — ECM Recall

### ✅ Engine Control Module Recall *(2022)*
- NHTSA recall affecting some 2022 Sierra HD units
- ECM software issue could cause unintended engine behaviors
- **Fix:** ECM software reprogramming at dealer — free, quick
- Verify completion on any 2022 purchase

---

## 2023–2024 — Current Production

### ✅ L5P + CP3 + Allison 10L1000
- Best available drivetrain combination in the 1-ton class
- No known systemic first-year or ongoing issues in current production

### 🟡 Allison Fluid — Non-Negotiable Maintenance
- TranSynd fluid at correct intervals is the most important maintenance item
- Heavy tow: every 25,000 miles
- Normal use: every 50,000 miles

## AT4 Package — Known Issues Specific to Sierra AT4

| Item | Issue | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| DSSV Dampers | Higher replacement cost at high mileage | Excellent when new; budget for 150K+ replacement |
| AT4 Lift Height | Ball mount drop required for some trailers | ~$30 hardware fix, not a structural concern |
| AT4 Skid Plates | Inspection recommended after off-road use | Rock strikes can dent/crack skid plates; check and replace if compromised |
| DuraTrac Tires | Wear faster than street tires | Factory Goodyear DuraTrac tires run ~50,000–60,000 miles; plan for replacement |

None of these are serious mechanical issues — they are ownership realities of the AT4 package. The DSSV damper cost is the most significant long-term expense unique to AT4 ownership.

## Summary: Worst Years vs. Best Years

| Category | Worst Years | Best Years |
|---|---|---|
| Injection pump | **2011–2016 LML (CP4.2)** ⚠️ | 2017+ L5P (CP3) ✅ |
| Water pump | 2017–2018 | 2019+ |
| EGR system | 2011–2014 | 2017+ |
| AT4 available? | 2011–2019 (NO AT4) | **2020+ only** |
| Allison 10-speed | All years (with correct fluid) | 2022+ (TSBs resolved) |
| DEF system | 2013–2016 | 2020+ |
| Overall | 2011–2014 | **2020+ AT4 (if budget allows)** |

**Best new buy in the entire truck comparison:** GMC Sierra 3500 AT4 (2020+) — CP3 + Allison + factory off-road package. The research points here for the stated use case of towing a travel trailer with dispersed off-road camping.

**Best used buy:** 2017–2019 L5P Sierra 3500 (no AT4 available, but CP3 + Allison on a depreciated truck). Or 2022+ AT4 if budget allows and you want the full package.

**Avoid:** Any 2011–2016 LML without CP3 conversion completed and documented.
