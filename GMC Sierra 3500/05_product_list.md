# GMC Sierra 3500 HD AT4 — Product List
*Recommended add-ons for towing a travel trailer + dispersed camping use*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> **Priority rating:** 🔴 Essential — do this before towing seriously | 🟠 Highly recommended | 🟡 Nice to have | ⬜ Optional/situational

> **AT4 advantage:** The Sierra AT4 comes with Goodyear DuraTrac AT tires, skid plates, and DSSV suspension from the factory. Those are three major items the Silverado, Ram, and F-350 (non-Tremor) don't have. The product list here is shorter as a result.

---

## Fuel System

### 🟡 Fuel Lubricity Additive *(L5P, 2017+ — nice to have)*

The CP3 pump on the L5P is significantly more tolerant of US diesel quality than the CP4. Still beneficial for injector cleanliness.

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Power Service Diesel Kleen (silver bottle) | ~$12–$15/quart | Good all-around choice |
| Stanadyne Performance Formula | ~$10–$14/quart | Solid option |

**If buying LML (2011–2016 Sierra, no AT4 available):** Additive becomes 🔴 Essential, same as Ford.

## Exhaust Brake

### 🟠 Aftermarket Exhaust Brake

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| BD Diesel Exhaust Brake (L5P Duramax) | ~$600–$900 installed | Best-known Duramax option |
| Banks Power Brake | ~$500–$800 installed | Works with Banks iDash gauge system |

The Allison 10L1000 in Tow/Haul mode provides meaningful engine braking — more than the Ford TorqShift baseline — but not equivalent to the Cummins exhaust brake. For mountain towing on serious grades, the exhaust brake is the right call.

### 🔴 Weight Distribution Hitch

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Equal-i-zer 4-Point Sway Control (10,000/1,000) | ~$900–$1,100 | Best-in-class; WDH + sway control in one |

**AT4 note:** The AT4's ~2" lift raises the hitch receiver height. Measure trailer coupler height vs. receiver height before ordering a ball mount — you'll likely need a **drop** ball mount rather than a rise. A 2" drop typically handles it (~$25–$40 for a quality drop mount).

### 🟡 Skid Plate Inspection / Replacement *(AT4 factory plates)*

The AT4's factory skid plates are steel and durable, but rock strikes can dent or crack them. Inspect after any rocky terrain. Replacement plates from GM are ~$300–$600 per section. Aftermarket options exist from Rough Country and SDHQ.

---

### 🟡 Tire Pressure Management System *(TPMS monitoring for dispersed camping)*

When airing down for off-road traction (25–30 PSI), factory TPMS will alarm. A manual gauge is all you need — pull the warning or get used to it. More importantly:

- **Air compressor for re-inflation** before returning to highway:

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| VIAIR 400P Portable Compressor | ~$120–$150 | Fast reinflation; well-proven for 10-ply LT tires |
| ARB Twin Air Compressor | ~$400–$500 | High end; faster, harder, better for heavy-duty use |

**Recommendation:** VIAIR 400P handles LT285/75R18 reinflation well within 10 minutes per tire. The ARB is faster but premium-priced.

### ⬜ Rear Differential Locker Upgrade *(if not G80 equipped)*

The G80 auto-locker is available but not standard on all Sierra 3500 trims. If your truck doesn't have it:

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ARB Air Locker (GM 11.5" AAM) | ~$800–$1,200 installed | Selectable locker; requires air compressor |
| Eaton E-Locker (GM 11.5" AAM) | ~$700–$1,000 installed | Electric selectable locker |

For dispersed camping use, this is 🟠 Highly Recommended if the truck doesn't have G80.

## Suspension / Leveling

### 🟡 Rear Air Bag Assist *(DSSV suspension + tongue weight)*

The AT4 DSSV suspension handles tongue weight better than standard shocks. Air bag assist may still be useful if you're running near the upper end of payload capacity.

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Air Lift LoadLifter 5000 (rear) | ~$300–$400 + installation | Adjustable; levels under load |
| Firestone Ride-Rite | ~$300–$400 + installation | Good alternative |

**Priority for AT4:** Lower than other trucks — the DSSV suspension handles sag better.
