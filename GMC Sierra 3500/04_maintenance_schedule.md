# GMC Sierra 3500 HD — Maintenance Schedule
*6.6L Duramax L5P V8 · Allison 10L1000 · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> **Platform note:** The Sierra 3500 HD and Silverado 3500 HD share an identical drivetrain. All maintenance intervals, fluid specs, and capacities are the same. Sierra-specific items (AT4 DSSV dampers, DuraTrac tires) are noted where they differ.

> **Towing context:** All intervals assume regular towing use (10,000–14,000 lb trailer, mixed highway/dispersed camping). Under tow load = severe duty.

---

## Engine Oil

| Item | Spec | Interval (towing) | Interval (normal) | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Engine oil | 0W-20 dexos D | **5,000–7,500 mi** | 7,500 mi | ~8 qts w/ filter |

**Recommended oils:**
- ACDelco dexos D 0W-20 (OEM)
- Shell Rotella T6 5W-40 (widely used by towing owners)
- Mobil Delvac 1 ESP 5W-40

**Notes:**
- 8 quarts total capacity — significantly less than Cummins (12 qts); don't overfill
- Use dexos D (diesel spec) — not dexos1 (gas spec)
- Always change filter with oil

---

## Fuel Filters

| Item | Interval (towing) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fuel filter | **15,000 mi** | Single spin-on filter |

**Notes:**
- L5P uses CP3 injection pump — less critical than CP4 trucks, but maintain on schedule
- ACDelco OEM filters maintain correct micron rating
- Drain water separator at each filter change

---

## Allison Transmission Fluid

### 10L1000 (2020+)
| Item | Spec | Interval (towing) | Interval (normal) | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Trans fluid | **Allison TranSynd 668** | **25,000 mi** | 45,000–50,000 mi | ~16 qts |

**Notes:**
- TranSynd is the only fluid Allison specifies. No substitutes.
- Available at truck stops, Allison dealers, and online
- Pan drop + filter — not a full flush
- The Allison's exceptional longevity is largely a result of correct fluid maintenance. This is the one item not to shortcut.

## AT4-Specific: DSSV Dampers

| Item | Interval | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| DSSV shock inspection | Every 50,000 mi | Check for leaking, collapsed |
| DSSV shock replacement | 100,000–150,000 mi typical life | ~$400–$600 each — budget accordingly |

**Notes:**
- DSSV dampers are Multimatic units — not serviceable, only replaceable
- More expensive than standard shocks but significantly better performance
- Check Multimatic's direct-sales pricing before going to dealer — often cheaper

---

## AT4-Specific: DuraTrac Tires

| Item | Interval | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tire rotation | Every 7,500–10,000 mi | Critical for even wear on AT tires |
| Tire replacement | ~50,000–65,000 mi | Goodyear DuraTrac life range |
| Tire pressure | Monthly / before each tow | AT tires should match door jamb spec; adjust down for off-road |

**Notes:**
- DuraTrac tires wear faster than all-season highway tires — budget for replacement
- For off-road dispersed camping: dropping pressure to 25–30 PSI dramatically improves traction on dirt/sand/rock (remember to re-inflate before highway driving)
- Replacement cost: ~$250–$350 per tire for LT285/75R18 DuraTrac

## Brake Service

| Item | Interval | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Brake fluid flush | Every 2 years | |
| Front brake pads | Inspect at 25,000 mi | |
| Rear brake pads | Inspect at 30,000 mi | |

**Notes:**
- No factory exhaust brake on Duramax — Allison Tow/Haul provides meaningful engine braking
- Aftermarket exhaust brake available (BD Diesel) for mountain towing — worthwhile if you regularly descend long grades with a loaded trailer

---

## Annual Inspection Checklist

- [ ] All fluid levels (oil, coolant, DEF, Allison, diffs, transfer case)
- [ ] Allison fluid condition (red/pink = good, dark = change)
- [ ] DEF system function test
- [ ] DuraTrac tire wear inspection (all four corners)
- [ ] AT4 skid plate integrity (inspect for rock damage)
- [ ] DSSV damper visual inspection (leaks, collapsed)
- [ ] EGR cooler / coolant level check
- [ ] Hitch receiver inspection
- [ ] Brake controller calibration check

## Estimated Annual Maintenance Cost (15,000 miles/year, regular towing)

| Service | Frequency | Est. Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Oil changes | 2× per year | ~$80–$120 |
| Fuel filter | 1× per year | ~$40–$70 |
| DEF fluid | Ongoing | ~$50–$80/year |
| Allison fluid + filter | Every 2 years (towing) | ~$150–$250 |
| Diff/transfer case service | Every 2 years | ~$150–$250 |
| DuraTrac tires (amortized) | Every 4–5 years | ~$120–$180/year |
| DSSV dampers (amortized) | Every 8–10 years | ~$80–$120/year |
| Misc. | Annual | ~$100–$150 |
| **Estimated annual total** | | **~$770–$1,220** |

> AT4 runs slightly higher than base Sierra/Silverado due to DuraTrac tire replacement cycle and DSSV damper amortization. The difference is modest — about $200/year over a standard Sierra.
