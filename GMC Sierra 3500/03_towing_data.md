# GMC Sierra 3500 HD — Towing Data
*6.6L Duramax L5P V8 · Allison 10L1000 · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> **Platform note:** The Sierra 3500 HD and Silverado 3500 HD share identical tow ratings across all configurations. Same engine, same transmission, same axles, same GCWR. All towing data here applies equally to both. Sierra-specific notes are called out where relevant.

> **How to read towing charts:** Published max ratings are always for the most favorable configuration under ideal conditions — correct axle ratio, max tow package, right transmission. The door jamb sticker on your specific truck is the only number that matters for your rig.

## What Actually Determines Your Rating

### Axle Ratio
- **3.73 axle:** Required for max tow rating. Standard with Max Trailering Package on Duramax.
- **3.42 axle:** Base configuration — significantly lower capacity.

### Max Trailering Package
- 3.73 rear axle
- Integrated brake controller
- Upgraded cooling
- Class V 2.5" receiver hitch

### Payload (SRW, Crew Cab, 4x4, Duramax)
- **Typical range:** ~3,500–4,500 lbs depending on trim and options
- **AT4 trim:** Falls toward middle of range (~3,800–4,200 lbs) due to DSSV dampers, skid plates, AT tires
- **Denali:** Similar range to AT4; heavier content equipment

> Always check the door jamb sticker on your specific truck.

## AT4-Specific Towing Notes

### Hitch Height
The AT4's ~2" lift raises the hitch point vs. a standard Sierra. Most travel trailers won't need a rise ball mount — you'll typically need a **drop** mount. Measure:
- Hitch receiver height from ground
- Trailer coupler height when level
- The difference = required drop

A 2" drop ball mount covers most AT4 situations (~$25–$40). Not a concern — just measure before you hitch up the first time.

### DSSV Suspension Under Tow Load
Owner feedback on the AT4's Multimatic DSSV dampers while towing is consistently positive:
- Less bounce transmission from trailer to truck vs. standard shocks
- Better tracking stability on washboard or chip seal highway
- Self-leveling characteristics reduce sag under tongue weight
- Some owners note improved crosswind stability vs. softer suspension trucks

### DuraTrac Tires and Towing
The factory Goodyear Wrangler DuraTrac tires on the AT4 have a higher load rating than passenger-car-style tires. They're a solid all-terrain tire for dispersed camping. Slight increase in road noise vs. all-season at highway speeds — not significant for most towing use.

## Electronic Rear Locker (G80) and Towing

The G80 auto-locker is available on AT4 (and other Sierra 3500 trims). Engages automatically under wheel slip, disengages above ~25 mph. Useful for:
- Launching on a wet boat ramp with a loaded trailer
- Pulling out of a soft-ground dispersed campsite
- Snow/ice departure on grades

Does not affect tow ratings.

## Hitch Receiver

- **Class V 2.5" receiver** standard with Max Trailering Package
- **Integrated brake controller:** Standard on SLT and above; available on lower trims
- **Tow/Haul mode:** Tightens Allison shift points, enables auto-grade braking, sharpens downshift response. Always use when towing.

---

## Real-World Towing Notes

- **Allison 10L1000 in Tow/Haul:** Widely regarded as the best towing transmission in the 1-ton segment. Holds gears confidently on grades, smooth transitions under load, manages descent with engine braking.
- **No dedicated exhaust brake:** Allison's transmission braking is meaningful but not equivalent to the Cummins exhaust brake. Aftermarket options available (BD Diesel). For mountain towing, plan for more brake use than a Ram.
- **DPF regen management:** Extended highway pulls at 60–65 mph reduce regen frequency. Avoid stop-and-go towing immediately before a long pull.
- **Fuel economy towing:** Expect 9–13 MPG depending on trailer, speed, and terrain.
