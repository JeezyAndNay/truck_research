# Ram 3500 HD — Known Issues by Year
*6.7L Cummins I6 · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> **Pump note:** The Ram 3500 Standard Output uses the CP3 injection pump on all years — a relatively forgiving failure mode. The High Output (2019+) uses a CP4.2 — when it fails, it contaminates the entire fuel system. Year-specific CP4 recall details are in the 2019–2020 section below.

## 2010–2012 — EGR Era (Pre-DEF)

The 6.7L Cummins launched in 2007.5. By 2010–2012, it was in its second revision — DPF and EGR equipped, but no DEF/SCR system yet.

### 🟠 Grid Heater Fastener Failure *(all years, worst in cold climates)*
- Internal fasteners arc and corrode, potentially breaking off into the intake manifold
- Symptoms: weak cold starts, intake-related DTCs, P0380
- All 6.7L Cummins years affected (2007.5–present)
- **Fix:** BD Diesel Killer Grid Heater (~$250). Preventive replacement recommended at 100K mi

### 🟠 EGR Cooler Clogging & Failure
- High EGR flow rates accumulate soot in the cooler passages
- Causes elevated coolant temps, white smoke, coolant loss
- Most common with short-trip driving or lots of idling
- **Fix:** EGR cooler replacement (~$400–$800 parts + labor). EGR delete not legal on road vehicles.

### 🟠 VGT Turbocharger Actuator Failure
- The variable geometry turbocharger uses an electric actuator to control vane position
- Soot buildup on vanes causes actuator to over-work and fail
- Codes: P003A (Actuator "A" Position Control), U010C, P0299 (underboost)
- Symptoms: turbo lag, reduced power, limp mode
- **First observed widely in 2013–2014; extended warranty issued in 2015**
- **Fix:** Actuator replacement ($800–$1,500). Manual vane cleaning at intervals helps prevent it.

### 🟡 68RFE Transmission — Torque Converter Shudder
- The 68RFE (standard auto in non-Aisin builds) has a marginal torque converter lockup under sustained heavy towing
- Shudder at 40–55 mph, especially when locked up under load
- Not a catastrophic failure — but a long-term wear concern under heavy tow use
- **Fix:** Transmission fluid change with appropriate friction modifier, or converter replacement if advanced (~$500–$2,000)

## 2013–2015 — DEF System Introduction

2013 was the first year the 6.7L Cummins added SCR (Selective Catalytic Reduction) and the DEF (Diesel Exhaust Fluid) system. Early DEF system reliability was not strong.

### 🟠 DEF Pump & Dosing Module Failures
- DEF pump failures caused limp mode and reduced-power events
- DEF injector (doser) would crystallize from AdBlue residue and fail
- Codes: P20EE (SCR NOx Catalyst Efficiency), P203F (DEF Level Sensor)
- Common in 2013 and 2014 model years
- **Fix:** DEF pump replacement (~$400–$800). Keeping DEF topped off and using quality fluid reduces crystallization.

### 🟠 NOx Sensor Failure
- Both upstream and downstream NOx sensors failed on early DEF-equipped trucks
- Triggers reduced power, DERATE warnings, eventual limp mode
- Accelerated by short-trip cycles (sensors don't reach full operating temp)
- **Fix:** Sensor replacement (~$200–$500). Use truck on highway drives to maintain sensor temps.

### 🟡 DEF Tank Heater Failure *(cold climates)*
- DEF freezes at 12°F. The factory tank heater prevents this.
- Heater failures in 2013–2015 cause DEF freeze and system shutdown in winter
- **Fix:** Tank heater replacement (~$300–$600)

### ✅ VGT Actuator Extended Warranty (2015 Campaign)
- Ram/Cummins issued an extended warranty for VGT actuator failures on 2007.5–2012 6.7L Cummins trucks
- Extended coverage: 15 years / 150,000 miles from original purchase
- If your 2010–2012 is within that window, actuator failure should be covered — verify at dealer

### 🟡 EGR Valve Sticking
- EGR valve deposits cause sticking and hesitation at light throttle
- Codes: P0401 (EGR Flow Insufficient), P0404
- **Fix:** EGR valve cleaning or replacement (~$200–$500)

## 2016–2018 — Mature Platform, Persistent Issues

The 5th-gen Ram 3500 (2013–2018 body) was well-developed by this point. Core issues are management, not structural.

### 🟠 VGT Actuator Failure *(ongoing)*
- VGT actuator wear continues to be the #1 known Cummins issue
- Now out of any extended warranty coverage
- **Fix:** Replacement + vane cleaning ($800–$1,500)

### 🟡 68RFE Converter Shudder *(ongoing)*
- Still the weak point of non-Aisin builds
- 2016–2018 trucks used under heavy tow loads show higher 68RFE failure rates than Aisin-equipped builds
- **Recommendation:** If buying a 2013–2018 used Ram 3500 for heavy towing, strongly prefer Aisin-equipped trucks

### 🟡 DEF System Maintenance Costs
- By 2016–2018, DEF system components are aging out of warranty
- Out-of-pocket DEF system repairs ($400–$1,500+) are common
- **Mitigation:** Maintain DEF fluid quality, keep tank above 1/3 full, run highway miles regularly

### 🟡 Grid Heater Fastener *(ongoing)*
- Still present on all years through present
- By 150K+ miles, this is an increasing failure risk

## 2019–2020 — New Body, CP4 Risk Arrives

2019 brought the 6th-gen Ram 3500 with a completely new body style and interior. Also introduced: the High Output Cummins — and with it, the CP4.2 injection pump.

### 🔴 CP4.2 High-Pressure Fuel Pump — Both SO and HO
- **Affected:** ALL 2019–2020 Ram 3500 with 6.7L Cummins — both Standard Output and High Output
- Both SO and HO switched to the Bosch CP4.2 for 2019–2020 (different part numbers: SO uses 0445124047, HO uses 0445124039 — same pump design and failure mode)
- The CP4.2 runs on marginal lubrication. US diesel fuel's lower sulfur content accelerates wear.
- **Failure mode:** Metal shrapnel contaminates injectors, fuel rails, fuel lines, and fuel tank
- **Cost of failure:** $8,000–$20,000 for complete fuel system replacement
- **NHTSA Investigation:** Opened. Multiple class-action lawsuits filed (similar to Ford CP4 suits).
- **Ram issued a recall:** FCA reference Y78 — covered all 2019–2020 Ram 2500/3500 with 6.7L Cummins (both SO and HO), 222,000+ vehicles. **Remedy was replacement of the CP4.2 with a CP3 pump.** Ram replaced the entire pump, not a filtration add-on.
- **2021 and later:** Ram removed the CP4.2 from ALL Cummins trucks (SO and HO) and reverted to CP3 across the board. 2025+ uses the new Bosch CP8 pump.

> **Buying a used 2019–2020 Ram 3500 (any output level)?** Both SO and HO had CP4 those years. Confirm the recall remedy has been applied — VIN lookup at nhtsa.gov. If unverified, treat it as a CP4 truck and price accordingly. **This applies to Standard Output too** — not just HO. Buying 2021+? CP3 is back on all engines.

### 🟡 First-Year 6th Gen Build Quality Issues *(2019)*
- Some 2019s had interior fit/finish complaints and minor electronic gremlins as expected with a first-year body
- No major powertrain first-year issues beyond the CP4 recall above

### 🟡 Air Suspension Issues *(if equipped — Limited/Longhorn 2019+)*
- Optional air suspension (AirRide) on higher trims saw compressor failures at higher mileages
- **Fix:** Compressor replacement (~$400–$900)

## 2021–2024 — Current Generation

### 🟡 CP4 on HO — Still the Risk *(2021+)*
- The HO Cummins CP4 concern did not go away with the recall remedy
- Preventive measure: many owners add a FASS or AirDog fuel system (pre-filters water and air, reduces pump cavitation)
- Standard Output trucks are unaffected (CP3)

### 🟡 VGT Actuator *(ongoing on all years)*
- Still the #1 recurring maintenance item on all 6.7L Cummins trucks

### 🟡 DEF System *(ongoing)*
- All DEF system components still require maintenance and will fail
- By 2021–2024, system design is mature but not immune

## Summary: Worst Years vs. Best Years

| Category | Worst Years | Best Years |
|---|---|---|
| Overall reliability | 2013–2015 (early DEF), 2019–2020 HO | 2016–2018 SO |
| Fuel system risk | 2019–2024 HO (CP4) | Any SO year (CP3) |
| Transmission | Any 68RFE under heavy tow | Aisin-equipped builds |
| DEF system | 2013–2015 | 2018+ (mature design) |
| VGT turbo | All years (maintenance item) | N/A — plan for it |

**Best used buy:** 2016–2018 Standard Output with Aisin transmission. CP3, no CP4 risk, mature DEF system, Aisin is bulletproof. Sweet spot for value and reliability.

**Avoid:** 2019–2020 HO unless recall confirmed applied and you add aftermarket fuel filtration.
