# Ram 3500 HD — Product List

## Fuel System

### 🟠 Fuel Lubricity Additive *(SO all years; ALL years if 2019–2020 with unverified recall)*
Even with a CP3 pump, a lubricity additive protects the entire fuel system and injectors — especially important if running low-sulfur diesel from off-brand stations near dispersed camping areas.

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Power Service Diesel Kleen + Cetane Boost (silver bottle) | ~$12–$15/quart | 1 qt treats ~100 gal. Best for active towing. |
| Stanadyne Performance Formula | ~$10–$14/quart | OEM-endorsed by Cummins. Good all-around choice. |
| Hot Shot's Secret Diesel Extreme | ~$20–$25 (treats 150 gal) | Deep-clean treat; use periodically |

**Recommendation:** Power Service silver bottle or Stanadyne at every fill-up. Not optional on a 2019–2020 if CP4 recall isn't confirmed.

---

### 🟠 Upgraded Fuel Filtration *(2019–2020 trucks; advisable on all)*

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| FASS Titanium Series (150 GPH) | ~$600–$700 | Industry standard. Removes air, water, particulates before CP3/CP4. |
| AirDog II-4G (165 GPH) | ~$550–$650 | Direct competitor to FASS. Also excellent. |

**Recommendation:** If running a 2019–2020 Ram diesel (whether recall was completed or not), add FASS or AirDog. On older CP3 trucks, still beneficial but not as urgent.

## Transmission

### 🟠 External Transmission Cooler *(68RFE trucks especially)*

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| B&M SuperCooler | ~$80–$120 | Good entry-level additional cooler |
| Derale Series 8000 Stack Plate Cooler | ~$120–$180 | Better cooling capacity for sustained towing |
| Mishimoto Universal Transmission Cooler | ~$150–$200 | High quality, good fitment |

**Recommendation:** The 68RFE runs hot under sustained towing — an add-on cooler extends fluid life and transmission life. Aisin trucks have better heat management; still beneficial but less urgent.

---

### 🟡 Transmission Temp Gauge / Monitor

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Banks iDash DataMonster | ~$300 | Monitors trans temp, EGT, boost, DEF level, and more. Highly recommended. |
| Edge Insight CTS3 | ~$250–$300 | Similar multi-parameter monitor |
| ScanGauge D | ~$160 | Budget option; OBDII-based, limited parameters |

**Recommendation:** The Banks iDash is the best all-in-one monitor for a tow rig. Knowing transmission and EGT in real time is the early warning system for problems.

## Exhaust / Engine Management

### 🟠 Exhaust Brake *(Built in — use it)*
The Cummins has a built-in variable geometry turbo exhaust brake. No add-on needed. Enable it via the dash button before any downgrade. It's highly effective — most Ram 3500 owners rely on it almost exclusively for mountain descents.

### 🔴 Weight Distribution Hitch *(trailer tongue weight over 1,000 lbs)*

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|

---

### 🟠 Fifth-Wheel/Gooseneck Prep *(future-proofing)*

If you ever consider a 5th wheel trailer, add the factory-prep 5th wheel/gooseneck option at purchase. Adding it later is expensive. If already missing, aftermarket rail kits are available (~$400–$800).

---

|---|---|---|
