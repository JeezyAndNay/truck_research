# Ram 3500 HD — Maintenance Schedule
*6.7L Cummins I6 · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> **Towing context:** All intervals below assume regular towing use (10,000–14,000 lb trailer, mixed highway/dispersed camping). "Normal" factory intervals are for non-towing street use. Under tow load = severe duty. Use the shorter intervals below.

---

## Engine Oil

| Item | Spec | Interval (towing) | Interval (normal) | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Engine oil | 15W-40 diesel (API CK-4) | **5,000–7,500 mi** | 15,000 mi | 12 qts w/ filter |

**Recommended oils:**
- Shell Rotella T6 Full Synthetic 5W-40 (best cold-start protection, widely available)
- Mobil Delvac 1 ESP 5W-40
- Valvoline Premium Blue 15W-40 (OEM-approved for Cummins)

**Notes:**
- Cummins factory interval is 15,000 miles with oil life monitor — that's for highway-only, non-towing use
- Under regular towing, 5,000–7,500 miles is the correct interval
- Always use API CK-4 rated oil — older CI-4+ is acceptable but not preferred on newer emissions systems
- 12 quarts total (11 qts without filter change — always change the filter)

---

## Fuel Filters

| Item | Interval (towing) | Interval (normal) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary fuel filter | **15,000 mi** | 15,000–20,000 mi | More frequently in dusty/desert environments |
| Secondary fuel filter (water separator) | **15,000 mi** | 15,000–20,000 mi | Check/drain water separator monthly |

**Notes:**
- The Cummins has a dual fuel filter system — primary (coarse) and secondary (fine/water separator)
- Replace both at the same time
- Drain the water separator every 5,000 miles or when the warning light activates — water in diesel is a CP3/CP4 killer
- Use OEM or Fleetguard filters (Cummins OEM supplier)

---

## Transmission Fluid

### 68RFE (Standard Output)
| Item | Spec | Interval (towing) | Interval (normal) | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Trans fluid | Mopar ATF+4 | **30,000 mi** | 60,000 mi | ~14 qts |

- 68RFE runs hot under sustained towing — 30,000 mile interval under tow use
- Add an external transmission cooler if not equipped (see Products doc)
- Pan drop + filter change — not a flush

### Aisin AS69RC (High Output)
| Item | Spec | Interval (towing) | Interval (normal) | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Trans fluid | Aisin-specified ATF (check owner's manual) | **45,000 mi** | 60,000 mi | ~18 qts |

- Aisin runs cooler than 68RFE under load — less maintenance pressure
- Pan drop preferred over flush on used trucks

## Rear Differential (AAM 11.5")

| Item | Spec | Interval | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rear diff fluid | 75W-140 synthetic gear oil | 30,000 mi (towing) / 60,000 mi (normal) | ~3.5 qts |

**Notes:**
- The AAM 11.5" rear axle is large and robust
- If G80 anti-spin locker equipped: standard gear oil is fine, no additive needed (geared LSD vs. clutch-type)
- Check for weeping seals at each oil change

---

## Coolant

| Item | Spec | Interval | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Engine coolant | OAT (Organic Acid Technology) — Mopar Coolant or equivalent | Every 5 years or 150,000 mi | ~6 gallons |

- Cummins is picky about coolant additives — use OAT spec only, not universal green
- Check coolant concentration every season if dispersed camping in freezing temps
- Inspect hoses and clamps at coolant change

---

## DEF System

| Item | Interval | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| DEF refill | As needed (~1 gal per 300–500 miles) | Use Blue DEF or equivalent ISO 22241 certified fluid |
| DEF tank inspection | Annually | Inspect heater element connections in cold climates |
| NOx sensor check | When codes appear | P20EE, P203F are common |
| DEF pump | Replace when failed | ~$400–$800 |

**Notes:**
- Use quality DEF fluid — cheap or expired DEF crystallizes in the doser and kills the injector
- Keep tank above 1/3 full — running low repeatedly stresses the pump
- In winter storage: DEF freezes at 12°F, but the factory heater handles this if it's working

---

## Air Filter

| Item | Interval | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Engine air filter | 30,000 mi standard / 15,000 mi in dusty conditions | Paper OEM or K&N reusable |

- Dusty dispersed camping environments will clog air filters faster — check more frequently
- A dirty air filter measurably reduces power and fuel economy on a turbocharged diesel

---

## Coolant Filter (if equipped)

Some Cummins installations have an in-line coolant filter. Check owner's manual. If equipped, replace every 2 years.

---

## Brake Service

| Item | Interval | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Brake fluid flush | Every 2 years | Diesel trucks absorb moisture faster under heavy tow use |
| Front brake pads | Inspect at 20,000 mi | Front pads wear faster when towing |
| Rear brake pads/shoes | Inspect at 30,000 mi | Less load on rears |
| Brake rotors | Inspect at pad change | Machine if within spec, replace if not |

**Notes:**
- Trailer brake controller reduces truck brake wear significantly — set sensitivity correctly
- The Cummins exhaust brake reduces service brake usage on descents — pads last longer vs. non-exhaust-brake trucks

---

## Tire Rotation & Maintenance

| Item | Interval | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tire rotation | Every 7,500–10,000 mi | Important for even wear under tow load |
| Tire pressure check | Monthly / before each tow trip | Check cold; inflate to door jamb spec |
| Wheel torque check | After first 50 miles after wheel service | Re-torque to spec after loosening |

---

## Annual Inspection Checklist

- [ ] All fluid levels (oil, coolant, DEF, trans, diffs, transfer case)
- [ ] Fuel filter water separator drain
- [ ] Trailer hitch receiver inspection (cracks, corrosion)
- [ ] Ball mount tightness and ball torque
- [ ] Brake controller calibration
- [ ] Exhaust system inspection (manifold cracks, DPF condition)
- [ ] Boost/charge air intercooler hose inspection (look for cracks, loose clamps)
- [ ] VGT turbocharger vane cleaning or inspection (every 100K mi)
- [ ] Grid heater inspection (especially in cold climates)
- [ ] DEF doser/injector inspection
