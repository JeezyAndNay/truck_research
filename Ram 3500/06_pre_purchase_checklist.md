# Ram 3500 — Pre-Purchase Checklist
*6.7L Cummins · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Print or use on your phone at the seller's location*

---

## Before You Go — Documentation

- [ ] Run VIN on [nhtsa.gov/recalls](https://www.nhtsa.gov/vehicle/recalls) — check for open recalls
- [ ] Run VIN on [vpic.nhtsa.dot.gov/decoder](https://vpic.nhtsa.dot.gov/decoder/) — confirm engine, transmission, trim
- [ ] Check VIN position 8: **T = High Output, 6 = Standard Output**
- [ ] Pull Carfax or AutoCheck — accidents, flood, title issues, odometer rollback
- [ ] Request maintenance records (oil changes, fuel filters, transmission service)
- [ ] Ask for original window sticker or build sheet if seller has it

---

## Injection Pump — Most Important Item

- [ ] Confirm model year
- [ ] **2019–2020 truck:** verify CP4 recall completion at nhtsa.gov (FCA ref Y78). Both SO and HO had CP4 those years. Remedy was full pump replacement — ask for paperwork
- [ ] **2019–2020 without confirmed recall:** walk away or price in full CP4 risk + $600–$800 FASS lift pump
- [ ] **2013–2018 or 2021+:** CP3 confirmed — this is the correct pump, no further action needed

---

## Transmission ID

- [ ] Identify which transmission: **Aisin AS69RC** (HO) or **68RFE** (SO)
- [ ] Aisin: "AISIN" cast into housing; visually distinct pan shape. Confirms HO engine
- [ ] If 68RFE: inspect for delayed engagement, slipping, harsh shifts — these are warning signs above 150K mi
- [ ] Ask for transmission fluid change history — ATF+4 for 68RFE, Aisin-spec fluid for Aisin
- [ ] Check fluid condition yourself if accessible — should be red/pink, not dark or burnt-smelling

---

## Exterior

- [ ] Panel gaps consistent — uneven gaps suggest prior accident repair
- [ ] All four wheel arches: check for rust bubbling under paint
- [ ] Rocker panels: prod with a key or screwdriver if rusting — soft = structural rust
- [ ] Bed: check corners, floor, and tailgate for rust; check under bedliner if equipped
- [ ] Hitch receiver: no cracks at weld points, no severe rust inside tube
- [ ] 7-pin trailer plug: inspect pins, no bent/corroded contacts
- [ ] Tires: adequate tread depth (>4/32"), even wear across tread face, DOT date code under 6 years
- [ ] All lights functional: headlights, brake, reverse, turn signals, marker lights

---

## Under Hood

- [ ] **Engine oil:** pull dipstick — should be amber/light brown. Black = overdue. Milky/foamy = coolant contamination (serious)
- [ ] **Coolant:** check reservoir level and color — green or OAT (orange/red). Milky = head gasket concern
- [ ] Check around EGR cooler and hose connections for coolant weeps or staining
- [ ] Primary fuel filter housing: no leaks, no weeping at seals
- [ ] Water separator bowl (lower filter): look for water/sediment in bowl
- [ ] Battery terminals: no heavy corrosion, tight connections
- [ ] Grid heater wiring connections: check for corrosion at the large lugs on the intake manifold
- [ ] Turbo: look for oil weeping around turbo seals; wiggle shaft — minimal play is normal, excessive play is not
- [ ] VGT actuator: confirm vacuum/electrical connections to the turbo actuator are intact

---

## Under the Truck

- [ ] Frame rails: no visible cracks, no weld repairs, no heavy rust perforation
- [ ] Transfer case: no leaks, check drain plug area for fresh oil
- [ ] Front driveshaft U-joints: grab and twist — no slop, no play
- [ ] Rear driveshaft: same check
- [ ] Rear differential: no leaks at pinion seal or cover gasket
- [ ] Confirm no factory rear locker — Ram 3500 has anti-spin LSD only (not selectable)
- [ ] Exhaust system: no rust-through, no cracks at flex section or manifold
- [ ] DEF tank: no leaks, check DEF level access point
- [ ] Fuel tank(s): no rust, no leaks at sending unit or filler neck
- [ ] If skid plates equipped: check for impact damage, cracks, loose mounting

---

## Interior & Electronics

- [ ] Connect OBD scanner (or bring one) — check for stored DTCs before test drive
- [ ] Dash: no warning lights after engine warmup
- [ ] DEF gauge reading: confirm DEF level shown, no DEF system fault codes
- [ ] Boost gauge (if equipped or via scanner): reads 0 at idle, builds under throttle
- [ ] Air conditioning: blows cold
- [ ] Infotainment and backup camera: functional
- [ ] 4WD selector: test 4H and 4L engagement (on a safe surface)
- [ ] Seats, door seals, windows: check for water intrusion staining on headliner and carpet

---

## Test Drive — Minimum 20 Minutes, Mixed Speeds

- [ ] **Cold start:** starts within 1–2 glow plug cycles. No excessive white smoke after 2 minutes of running
- [ ] **Transmission:** smooth shifts through all gears, no slipping, no hunting between gears, no delayed engagement from stop
- [ ] **Turbo:** spools smoothly — listen for unusual whistling, rattling, or surging under load
- [ ] **Exhaust brake:** enable on a downgrade — confirm strong engine braking, no harsh engagement
- [ ] **Acceleration:** clean, linear power delivery. Hesitation or stumble can indicate injector issues
- [ ] **Highway cruise:** no vibrations, no wander, tracks straight
- [ ] **Braking:** firm pedal, no pulling left or right, no pulsating (warped rotors)
- [ ] **4WD:** engage 4H — no grinding, clunking, or hesitation. Engage 4L — same
- [ ] **No new warning lights** after 15+ minutes of driving

---

## Truck-Specific Watch Items by Mileage

| Mileage | Items to Scrutinize |
|---|---|
| Any | CP pump type, transmission type, maintenance records |
| 100K+ | VGT actuator function (hard start, power loss = suspect), injector balance rates via scanner |
| 150K+ | 68RFE condition (SO trucks) — slipping or harsh shifts = budget $2,500–$4,000 rebuild |
| 200K+ | Full pre-purchase inspection by diesel shop is mandatory |
| Any 2019–2020 | CP4 recall confirmation before any discussion of price |

---

## Mileage Assessment

- [ ] **Under 150K** — confident buy range for CP3 trucks (2013–2018 or 2021+)
- [ ] **150K–250K** — acceptable with documented maintenance history
- [ ] **250K+** — pre-purchase diesel shop inspection mandatory; budget for turbo actuator, injectors

---

## Pre-Purchase Inspection (any truck over 80K miles)

- [ ] Booked at a diesel-specialist shop ($150–$200)
- [ ] Requested: compression test, scanner live data review, physical lift inspection
- [ ] Injector balance rates within spec (no misfires, no high-compensation cylinders)
- [ ] No active or pending fault codes
- [ ] Technician comments reviewed

---

## Final Decision Gate

- [ ] CP pump type confirmed and acceptable ✓
- [ ] Transmission type confirmed and shift quality verified ✓
- [ ] No active DTCs ✓
- [ ] Maintenance records reviewed and adequate ✓
- [ ] Mileage within acceptable range for documentation level ✓
- [ ] Pre-purchase inspection passed (if applicable) ✓
- [ ] Price negotiated to reflect actual condition ✓

> **All boxes checked? You're ready to buy.**
> **Any unchecked? Negotiate the item into the price or walk.**
