# Ford F-350 — Pre-Purchase Checklist
*6.7L Power Stroke · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Print or use on your phone at the seller's location*

---

> **CP4 note:** Every 6.7L Power Stroke diesel ever built uses the CP4.2 injection pump. There is no year, trim, or configuration that avoids it. CP4 mitigation (lift pump + lubricity additive) is a permanent cost of ownership — confirm it was practiced before purchase.

---

## Before You Go — Documentation

- [ ] Run VIN on [nhtsa.gov/recalls](https://www.nhtsa.gov/vehicle/recalls) — check for open recalls
- [ ] Run VIN on [vpic.nhtsa.dot.gov/decoder](https://vpic.nhtsa.dot.gov/decoder/) — confirm year, engine, transmission, trim
- [ ] Pull Carfax or AutoCheck — accidents, flood, title issues, odometer rollback
- [ ] Request maintenance records — specifically fuel filter change intervals and additive use
- [ ] Ask if FASS or AirDog lift pump is installed (huge positive if yes)
- [ ] Ask for original window sticker or build sheet if available

---

## CP4 Pump — Critical Assessment

- [ ] Was a FASS Titanium or AirDog II-4G lift pump installed? (check under hood)
- [ ] Was a lubricity additive (Power Service Diesel Kleen, Stanadyne) used at every fill-up? Ask for receipts or seller confirmation
- [ ] Fuel filter change interval: every 10,000–15,000 miles? More frequent is better
- [ ] **No documented mitigation:** price the truck $1,000–$1,500 lower to cover immediate FASS install + additive budget
- [ ] **CP4 has already failed:** not a buy unless you're a diesel mechanic and the price reflects a full fuel system replacement ($8,000–$20,000 repair)

---

## Transmission — TorqShift

- [ ] 2011–2016: 6R140 6-speed. 2017+: 10R140 10-speed (early 10-speed had calibration issues — check for TSB history)
- [ ] Fluid change history: Ford spec Mercon LV, 30,000–45,000 mile tow intervals
- [ ] Shifts smoothly through all gears — no shudder, no harsh bang, no slipping
- [ ] No delayed engagement from stop or when selecting drive/reverse

---

## IWE System (4x4 Front Hubs) — Known Ford Issue

- [ ] Test 4WD engagement and disengagement: listen for grinding or clicking in front end
- [ ] Ask if IWE vacuum check valves have been replaced (common $20–$40 fix that prevents hub actuator failure)
- [ ] Any grinding noise in 4WD = suspect IWE actuators (~$200–$300 pair to replace)

---

## Exterior

- [ ] Panel gaps consistent — uneven gaps suggest prior accident repair
- [ ] All four wheel arches: check for rust bubbling under paint
- [ ] Rocker panels: check for rust, especially on 2011–2016 trucks
- [ ] Bed: check corners, floor, and tailgate for rust; check under bedliner if equipped
- [ ] Hitch receiver: no cracks at weld points, no severe rust inside tube
- [ ] 7-pin trailer plug: inspect pins, no bent/corroded contacts
- [ ] Tires: adequate tread depth (>4/32"), even wear, DOT date code under 6 years
- [ ] **Tremor:** factory Goodyear DuraTrac LT285/75R18 — verify originals are still on (or note what replaced them)
- [ ] All lights functional: headlights, brake, reverse, turn signals, marker lights

---

## Under Hood

- [ ] **Engine oil:** pull dipstick — should be amber/light brown. Black = overdue. Milky/foamy = coolant contamination (serious)
- [ ] **Coolant:** check reservoir level and color. Milky = head gasket concern
- [ ] **EGR cooler (2011–2014 trucks especially):** look for coolant staining or weeping around EGR cooler and at coolant degas bottle — this is the primary failure on early 6.7L trucks
- [ ] Fuel filter housing: no leaks at bowl or filter canister
- [ ] **Check for FASS/AirDog:** look for an aftermarket pump mounted to frame near fuel tank. This is the most important underhood/under-truck positive indicator
- [ ] **Crank damper (100K+ trucks):** inspect rubber isolator on crank pulley — cracking or separation means replacement due (~$300–$500 part)
- [ ] Battery terminals: clean and tight
- [ ] Turbo: no oil weeping around seals; shaft play check (wiggle compressor wheel — minimal play is normal)

---

## Under the Truck

- [ ] Frame rails: no visible cracks, weld repairs, or heavy rust perforation
- [ ] **FASS or AirDog lift pump:** look for aftermarket pump on frame rail near tank — major buying positive
- [ ] Transfer case: no leaks
- [ ] Front driveshaft and U-joints: check for play/wear
- [ ] Front differential: no leaks; check IWE actuators at each front hub
- [ ] Rear differential: no leaks at pinion seal or cover
- [ ] **Tremor:** check front diff locker actuator and rear e-locker actuator connections
- [ ] Exhaust: no rust-through, no cracks at DPF or exhaust manifold
- [ ] DEF tank: no leaks, DEF level accessible
- [ ] **Non-Tremor:** no skid plates standard — note if aftermarket plates are installed

---

## Interior & Electronics

- [ ] Connect OBD scanner — check for stored DTCs, especially fuel system or injector codes
- [ ] No warning lights after engine warmup
- [ ] DEF gauge reads correctly, no DEF fault codes
- [ ] Air conditioning: blows cold
- [ ] Infotainment and backup camera: functional
- [ ] Pro Trailer Backup Assist (2018+): confirm knob is present and functional if equipped
- [ ] 4WD selector: test 4H and 4L engagement
- [ ] **Tremor:** confirm front locker and rear e-locker engage/disengage correctly

---

## Test Drive — Minimum 20 Minutes, Mixed Speeds

- [ ] **Cold start:** clean start, no excessive white smoke after 2 minutes
- [ ] **Transmission:** all gears smooth, no shudder at light throttle (torque converter shudder), no hunting
- [ ] **Turbo:** smooth spool, no unusual whistling or surging
- [ ] **No exhaust brake** on Power Stroke — normal; budget for aftermarket if mountain towing
- [ ] **Regen cycle:** if truck initiates a regen during drive, watch for fuel dilution warning — this is a known 6.7L issue
- [ ] **Fuel dilution check:** ask when last oil change was. High fuel dilution (from frequent short-trip regens) shows up as oil level rising on dipstick and diesel smell in oil
- [ ] **Highway cruise:** no vibrations, no wander
- [ ] **Braking:** firm pedal, no pulling, no pulsating
- [ ] **4WD:** 4H and 4L — no grinding, no front-end clunking
- [ ] **No new warning lights** after 15+ minutes of driving

---

## Truck-Specific Watch Items by Mileage

| Mileage | Items to Scrutinize |
|---|---|
| Any | CP4 mitigation history — this is the #1 item regardless of mileage |
| Under 50K | Check for IWE issues (can appear early) |
| 2011–2014, any mileage | EGR cooler history — replacement is common in this range |
| 100K+ | Crank damper inspection; injector balance rates via scanner |
| 150K+ | Full pre-purchase diesel shop inspection is strongly recommended |

---

## Mileage Assessment

- [ ] **Under 100K without documented mitigation** — acceptable but add FASS immediately
- [ ] **Under 100K with documented FASS + additive** — confident buy
- [ ] **100K–130K with documented mitigation** — acceptable with inspection
- [ ] **130K+ without mitigation documentation** — significant CP4 risk; price accordingly or walk

---

## Pre-Purchase Inspection (any truck over 80K miles)

- [ ] Booked at a diesel-specialist shop ($150–$200)
- [ ] Requested: compression test, scanner live data, fuel system check, physical inspection
- [ ] Injector balance rates within spec
- [ ] No fuel dilution in oil (check oil on dipstick for diesel smell and level)
- [ ] EGR system condition checked

---

## Final Decision Gate

- [ ] CP4 mitigation history confirmed or FASS to be installed day one ✓
- [ ] Transmission shifts clean, no shudder or slipping ✓
- [ ] IWE system checked and functional ✓
- [ ] No active DTCs ✓
- [ ] EGR cooler history reviewed (2011–2014 trucks) ✓
- [ ] Mileage within acceptable range for documentation level ✓
- [ ] Pre-purchase inspection passed (if applicable) ✓
- [ ] Price negotiated to reflect condition ✓

> **All boxes checked? You're ready to buy — start the additive at the first fill-up.**
> **Any unchecked? Negotiate the item into the price or walk.**
