# Ford F-350 HD — Product List
*Recommended add-ons for towing a travel trailer + dispersed camping use*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> **Priority rating:** 🔴 Essential — do this before towing seriously | 🟠 Highly recommended | 🟡 Nice to have | ⬜ Optional/situational

> **CP4 note:** Two items on this list — fuel filtration and lubricity additive — are effectively mandatory on any used F-350 diesel. Budget for both before anything else.

---

## Fuel System — CP4 Mitigation (Non-Negotiable)

### 🔴 Fuel Lubricity Additive *(ALL F-350 diesel years — no exceptions)*

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Power Service Diesel Kleen + Cetane Boost (silver bottle) | ~$12–$15/quart | 1 qt treats ~100 gal. Best for active use. |
| Stanadyne Performance Formula | ~$10–$14/quart | |
| Hot Shot's Secret Diesel Extreme | ~$20–$25 (treats 150 gal) | Use periodically for deep clean |

**This is not optional.** The CP4 was designed for European diesel lubricity specs. US diesel is lower. Additive at every fill-up.

---

### 🔴 Upgraded Fuel Filtration *(ALL F-350 diesel — highest priority purchase)*

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| FASS Titanium Series (150 GPH) | ~$600–$700 | Removes air, water, particulates before they reach CP4 |
| AirDog II-4G (165 GPH) | ~$550–$650 | Direct competitor; equally well regarded |
| Fleece Performance FPE-KIT-P1567 | ~$350–$450 | Budget option; less water separation capability |

**Recommendation:** FASS or AirDog. The cost of a new CP4 failure dwarfs the cost of this system. This is the single most important dollar spent on a used Power Stroke.

## Exhaust Brake

### 🔴 Aftermarket Exhaust Brake *(All 6.7L Power Stroke — no factory exhaust brake)*

The Power Stroke has no factory exhaust brake. For mountain towing with a loaded trailer, this is a meaningful safety and brake wear gap vs. Ram.

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| BD Diesel Exhaust Brake (6.7L Power Stroke) | ~$600–$900 installed | Works with factory turbo; adds serious engine braking |
| Banks Power Brake Exhaust Brake | ~$500–$800 installed | Banks alternative; works with Banks gauge system |

**Recommendation:** If you're towing in mountainous terrain, an exhaust brake is the right call. If you're staying on relatively flat ground, it's optional — but brake pad costs will be higher without one.

## IWE Repair Kit *(Preventive — all Super Duty 4x4)*

### 🟠 IWE Vacuum Check Valve and Actuator Kit

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dorman IWE Repair Kit (vacuum check valve) | ~$20–$40 | Prevents backflow into front hub actuators |
| OEM IWE Actuator (pair) | ~$200–$300 | Replace when grinding occurs in 4WD |

The IWE system (front hub 4WD engagement) is a known recurring issue on all Super Duty 4x4s. Installing a check valve is cheap prevention; having the actuator part numbers on hand saves time when they fail.
