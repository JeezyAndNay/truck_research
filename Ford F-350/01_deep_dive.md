# Ford F-350 — Deep Dive
*6.7L Power Stroke V8 · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4 · Diesel*
*Research date: June 2026*

## The 6.7L Power Stroke at a Glance

The 6.7L Power Stroke is the first diesel engine Ford designed and built entirely in-house. Every previous Super Duty diesel (7.3L IDI, 7.3L Power Stroke, 6.0L, 6.4L) was either an International Navistar design or a joint program. The 6.7L is Ford's own work — a 90-degree V8, reverse-flow design with the turbo mounted in the valley between the cylinder banks.

The engine launched in 2011. Early years had significant reliability issues. By 2015 most were resolved. Post-2017 is the strong era.

| Year Range | Power | Torque | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2011–2014 | 390 hp | 735 lb-ft | First gen — EGR cooler failures, CP4 risk, avoid on used market |
| 2015–2016 | 440 hp | 860 lb-ft | EGR cooler redesigned. Significant reliability improvement |
| 2017–2019 | 450 hp | 935 lb-ft | Best of 3rd gen body. Mid-cycle refresh sorted most issues |
| 2020–2022 | 475 hp | 1,050 lb-ft | New body style, new 10-speed TorqShift. Highest ratings |
| 2023–2025 | 475 hp | 1,050 lb-ft | Current gen. Strong reliability. 7.3L Godzilla gas option added |

## Generations

### 5th Generation (2020–Present) — Current Body Style

**2020:** Complete redesign. New aluminum-intensive body. New 10-speed TorqShift transmission replaced the 6-speed TorqShift. Power jumped to 475 hp / 1,050 lb-ft. New interior, new tech stack. Pro Power Onboard (generator) available. 7.3L Godzilla V8 gas engine added as alternative.

**2021:** Minor updates. Sync 4 infotainment.

**2023:** Refresh. Updated front end, better interior materials, 12-inch touchscreen standard.

> **Best 5th gen:** 2021–2023. 2020 had first-year TorqShift 10-speed teething. 2021+ is more sorted.

### 4th Generation (2017–2019) — Bridge Gen

Same body as 2011–2016 but significantly refreshed. Power at 450 hp / 935 lb-ft. The most reliable era of the 3rd gen body style. EGR cooler issues from early production are resolved. Still carries CP4 risk.

> **Best 4th gen:** 2017–2019. Avoid 2011–2014 entirely for used buying.

### 3rd Generation (2011–2016) — The Troubled Early 6.7L

**2011–2014:** Avoid on used market. EGR cooler failures, CP4 pump risk, cooling system issues. These years have a documented failure pattern that's expensive to remediate.

**2015–2016:** EGR cooler redesign in 2015 resolved the main failure mode. If buying this range, verify the EGR cooler has been updated or replaced.

## Engine Deep Dive — 6.7L Power Stroke

**Configuration:** V8, 90-degree, turbocharged, intercooled
**Displacement:** 6.7L (409 cu in)
**Unique design:** Reverse-flow — intake on outside of V, exhaust in the valley. Turbo mounted in valley (hot side in center, away from bodywork). Compressor on driver's side.

### Reverse-Flow Design — Why It Matters

Most V8 engines put intake in the valley and exhaust on the outside. Ford reversed this. The result:
- Exhaust heat stays in the center of the engine, away from the cab and bodywork
- Turbo is physically protected in the valley
- Shorter intake paths → better throttle response
- The tradeoff: the turbo is harder to reach for service than a conventionally mounted turbo

### The CP4 Problem — All Years

Unlike Ram (which offers a CP3 option on the Standard Output), the 6.7L Power Stroke uses the CP4.2 injection pump on every build, every year since 2011. There is no CP3 option.

**What this means for used buying:**
- Every used Power Stroke diesel has CP4 risk
- The 2011–2019 Power Stroke has an active class action lawsuit history over CP4 failures
- Ford added additional fuel filtration improvements in 2020 to address the issue — but the pump itself is still CP4
- **Mitigation is mandatory:** Fuel lubricity additive (Power Service Diesel Kleen, Stanadyne) at every fill-up, or an aftermarket CP4 delete (replaces with CP3 — ~$2,000–$4,000, but see emissions note in comparison doc)

## Transmission — TorqShift 10R140 (2020+) and 6R140 (2011–2019)

### 10R140 (2020–Present)
- Ford-designed 10-speed SelectShift automatic
- Built specifically for Super Duty towing loads
- More closely spaced gear ratios = better tow management
- No major issues documented in the 2020+ fleet
- Fluid change: every 60,000 miles (or 30,000 with heavy towing)

### 6R140 (2011–2019)
- Ford-designed 6-speed SelectShift automatic
- Solid transmission — no major reliability issues noted
- Less sophisticated under load than the 10-speed but capable
- Fluid: same interval recommendations

## The Tremor Package — Off-Road F-350

The F-350 Tremor is Ford's factory off-road answer for the 1-ton class. Available on 2020+ F-350.

**What Tremor includes:**
- Bilstein monotube off-road shock absorbers (factory-tuned)
- Goodyear Wrangler DuraTrac LT285/75R18 tires (~35") from factory
- Skid plates (transfer case, front differential, engine)
- **Electronic-locking rear differential** — Torsen limited-slip front differential (**no front locker**)
- Raised suspension (+1" front, +0.5" rear vs. standard)
- Orange tow hooks, Tremor-specific styling
- Off-road trail control and rock crawl mode

**Tremor vs. Standard F-350:** Materially better off-road capability, factory-backed and warranted. For dispersed camping and backcountry access with a heavy trailer, the Tremor is the most capable F-350 configuration short of an aftermarket lift.

**Tremor tow ratings:** Identical to standard F-350 with same engine — the off-road upgrades don't reduce towing capacity.

## Honest Bottom Line

The F-350 Super Duty is the tow rating champion — 40,000 lbs puts it alone at the top of the class. Post-2017 reliability is genuinely strong; the early 6.7L problems are well behind Ford at this point.

**The CP4 is the only real knock.** It's on every Power Stroke ever built. If you're buying used, additive treatment from day one is not optional — it's the cost of doing business with a Ford diesel. If that gives you pause, buy a Duramax.

**Best used buy:** 2017–2019 F-350 diesel with documented fuel system history. Verify CP4 mitigation has been practiced (ask for fuel receipt patterns, additive history). Add Diesel Kleen to every tank starting day one.

**New:** 2020+ with the 10-speed TorqShift is a genuinely excellent truck. Highest tow ratings, strong engine, good electronics. Buy with confidence — just start the additive treatment immediately.

**Tremor:** If off-road capability matters, the Tremor is the strongest 1-ton factory off-road package alongside the GMC AT4. Worth the premium if you're going to use it.
