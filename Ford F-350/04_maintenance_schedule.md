# Ford F-350 HD — Maintenance Schedule
*6.7L Power Stroke V8 · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> **Towing context:** All intervals below assume regular towing use (10,000–14,000 lb trailer, mixed highway/dispersed camping). "Normal" factory intervals are for non-towing street use. Under tow load = severe duty. Use the shorter intervals below.

> **CP4 note:** The 6.7L Power Stroke uses the CP4.2 injection pump on all years. Fuel filter maintenance on this truck is not optional — a clogged or bypassed fuel filter accelerates CP4 wear. Stay on schedule.

---

## Engine Oil

| Item | Spec | Interval (towing) | Interval (normal) | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Engine oil | 10W-30 diesel (API CK-4) or 5W-40 | **5,000–7,500 mi** | 10,000 mi | ~13.1 qts w/ filter |

**Recommended oils:**
- Motorcraft SAE 10W-30 Super Duty Diesel Motor Oil (OEM)
- Shell Rotella T6 5W-40 Full Synthetic
- Mobil Delvac 1 ESP 5W-40

**Notes:**
- Ford's factory interval is 10,000 miles with Intelligent Oil-Life Monitor
- Under towing, 5,000–7,500 miles is the correct interval
- **Fuel dilution risk:** Under heavy DPF regen cycles (common in city driving), diesel can enter the crankcase. If oil level rises above the full mark or smells of diesel, change immediately regardless of mileage.
- 13.1 quarts total (always replace filter)

## Fuel Filters

| Item | Interval (towing) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Primary fuel filter | **10,000–15,000 mi** | OEM or Motorcraft recommended |
| Secondary fuel filter | **10,000–15,000 mi** | Replace both simultaneously |

**Notes:**
- **This is the most important maintenance item on a Power Stroke.** Contaminated or degraded fuel starves the CP4 pump and accelerates wear.
- Use Motorcraft OEM filters — they maintain the correct micron rating for the CP4
- Never run extended intervals on fuel filters with a CP4 pump
- If you add a FASS or AirDog fuel system (see Products doc), it includes a pre-filter that protects the CP4 — still replace factory filters on schedule
- Add a fuel lubricity additive (Power Service Diesel Kleen or Stanadyne Performance Formula) at every fill-up — this is the single most cost-effective CP4 protection

## Brake Service

| Item | Interval | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Brake fluid flush | Every 2 years | |
| Front brake pads | Inspect at 20,000 mi | Heavier wear without an exhaust brake |
| Rear brake pads | Inspect at 25,000 mi | |

**Notes:**
- The Power Stroke has **no factory exhaust brake** — service brakes carry more load on descents vs. a Cummins. More frequent pad inspection is warranted, especially if towing in mountainous terrain.
- Trailer brake controller reduces truck brake wear — calibrate sensitivity correctly

---

## IWE (Integrated Wheel End) 4WD Actuator

| Item | Interval | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| IWE inspection | Annually | Listen for grinding in 4WD; check vacuum lines |
| Vacuum check valve | Replace if grinding occurs | ~$20 part, prevents backflow into actuator |

The IWE is a known recurring maintenance item on Super Duty trucks. Grinding noise when engaging 4WD = inspect the IWE actuators and vacuum lines.

---

## Crankshaft Vibration Damper

| Item | Interval | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Crank damper inspection | Every oil change above 100,000 mi | Look for delamination, wobble |

The crank damper is a documented failure item on 2014–2019 Power Strokes. Inspect at every oil change at higher mileages; replace if any signs of delamination (~$300–$700).

---

## Annual Inspection Checklist

- [ ] All fluid levels
- [ ] Fuel filter water separator drain
- [ ] Fuel lubricity additive in tank
- [ ] Trailer hitch and ball mount inspection
- [ ] IWE actuator / 4WD engagement test
- [ ] EGR cooler leak check (white exhaust, coolant level)
- [ ] Crank damper visual inspection (100K+ mi trucks)
- [ ] Turbo coolant inlet fitting (plastic fitting — inspect for cracks, replace with billet if not done)
- [ ] Intercooler hose inspection
- [ ] Exhaust system / DPF pressure check
