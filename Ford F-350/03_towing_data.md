# Ford F-350 HD — Towing Data
*6.7L Power Stroke V8 · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> **How to read towing charts:** Published max ratings are always for the most favorable configuration under ideal conditions — correct axle ratio, max tow package, right transmission. Your actual truck may rate lower. The door jamb sticker on your specific truck is the only number that matters for your rig.

## What Actually Determines Your Rating

### Axle Ratio — The Critical Variable on F-350
The difference between the 4.30 and 3.73 axle on the F-350 is larger than on Ram or GM trucks. The 40,000 lb headline is 4.30 territory only.

- **4.30 axle:** Max tow package, unlocks full rating. Standard with Trailer Tow Package.
- **3.73 axle:** Most common on retail lot trucks without tow package. Good all-around gearing but meaningfully less tow capacity.
- **3.55 axle:** Base configuration. Not recommended for heavy towing.

### Trailer Tow Package
Required for max ratings on F-350. Includes:
- 4.30 rear axle
- Heavy-duty trailer tow mirrors
- Integrated trailer brake controller
- Class V 2.5" receiver
- Upgraded cooling system

### Payload (SRW, Crew Cab, 4x4)
The F-350 SRW crew cab payload varies widely by trim and options:
- **Max possible:** ~4,700 lbs (base trim, minimal options)
- **Typical Lariat/King Ranch crew cab:** ~3,500–4,200 lbs
- **Tremor or heavily optioned:** ~3,200–3,800 lbs

> **Always read the door jamb sticker** — payload is calculated for your specific build. Two identical-looking F-350s can have very different payload ratings based on options weight.

**Travel trailer at 10,000–14,000 lbs:**

| Metric | Your Trailer | F-350 (diesel, 4.30) | Headroom |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tow rating | 10,000–14,000 lbs | 40,000 lbs | Substantial |

The F-350's 40,000 lb rating is more capacity than you'll ever use for a travel trailer. The truck is overpowered for the stated use case — which is fine. The real question is used market CP4 risk and reliability, not tow rating.

## Hitch Receiver

- **Class V 2.5" receiver** standard on 3500 trims with Trailer Tow Package
- **Ford integrated brake controller:** Standard on most XLT and up, required for trailers with brakes
- **Pro Trailer Backup Assist:** Available on 2018+ — rotates trailer using a knob instead of steering wheel. Genuinely useful for maneuvering in tight campsites.

---

## Tremor — Towing Impact

The Tremor package does not reduce tow ratings. Same max capacity as a standard F-350 with equivalent powertrain. The ~1" suspension lift does raise the hitch point slightly — may require a ball mount with more drop to match trailer coupler height. This is a $30 fix, not a structural concern.

---

## Real-World Towing Notes

- **No factory exhaust brake on Power Stroke:** Unlike the Cummins, the 6.7L Power Stroke does not have a built-in exhaust brake. Engine braking is limited. An aftermarket exhaust brake (BD Diesel, Banks) is recommended for mountain towing with a heavy trailer.
- **Fuel dilution under regen:** DPF active regen can introduce diesel into the crankcase during towing. More frequent oil changes (every 5,000–7,500 miles) are recommended vs. the standard interval.
- **10-speed shift behavior under tow load:** The 10R140 is well-calibrated for towing — holds gears on grades better than the old 6-speed. Enable Tow/Haul mode to tighten shift points and increase engine braking through downshifts.
- **Fuel economy towing:** Expect 9–13 MPG depending on trailer, speed, and grade.
