# Cross-Brand Comparison — 1-Ton Diesel SRW
*Ram 3500 · Ford F-350 · Chevy Silverado 3500 · GMC Sierra 3500*
*Research date: June 2026*

> **Use case:** Towing a travel trailer (targeting 10,000–14,000 lbs), dispersed off-grid camping, daily driving, long-term ownership. All configurations: crew cab, SRW, 4x4, diesel.

---

## The One Thing That Matters Most on the Used Market

**Injection pump type: CP3 vs. CP4**

This is the single most important factor when buying a used diesel truck in this class. Get it wrong and you're looking at a $10,000–$20,000 repair.

| Truck | Years | Pump | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ram 3500 Cummins (SO + HO) | 2007.5–2018 | CP3 | ✅ Low — all Cummins used CP3 through entire 4th gen |
| Ram 3500 Cummins (SO + HO) | **2019–2020** | **CP4.2** | 🔴 High — **both SO and HO** had CP4 (different part numbers, same failure risk). Recall issued; remedy was CP3 replacement. Verify on any 2019–2020. |
| Ram 3500 Cummins (SO + HO) | **2021–2024** | CP3 | ✅ Low — Ram reverted ALL Cummins back to CP3 for 2021+ |
| Ram 3500 Cummins (SO + HO) | **2025+** | CP8 | ⚠️ New Bosch CP8 pump; limited field history |
| Ford F-350 Power Stroke 6.7L | **2011–2019** | CP4.2 | 🔴 High — class action settlement filed |
| Ford F-350 Power Stroke 6.7L | 2020–present | CP4.2 | 🔴 High — still CP4, Ford added fuel filter improvements |
| Chevy/GMC Duramax LML | **2011–2016** | **CP4.2** | 🔴 High — same risk as Ford; CP3 conversion strongly advised |
| Chevy/GMC Duramax L5P | **2017–present** | CP3 | ✅ Low — CP3 returned with L5P; safest used diesel fuel system |

**What CP4 failure means:** The CP4.2 Bosch pump runs on diesel fuel for lubrication. US diesel is low-lubricity compared to European diesel the pump was designed for. When the pump fails, metal shavings contaminate the entire fuel system — injectors, lines, fuel rail, everything. Remediation cost: $8,000–$20,000+. Not covered under most warranties once the factory warranty expires.

**Bottom line:** If you buy any used Ram from 2019–2020 (SO or HO), any used Ford Super Duty diesel, or a used Chevy/GMC with the LML Duramax (2011–2016), CP4 risk is present. The safe used buys are: Ram (2007.5–2018 OR 2021–2024), Chevy/GMC L5P (2017+). The safe Duramax is L5P only — LML is CP4 same as Ford.

## Engine Comparison

| | Ram 3500 Cummins | Ford F-350 Power Stroke | Chevy/GMC Duramax |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Configuration** | I6 (inline 6-cylinder) | V8 | V8 |
| **Displacement** | 6.7L | 6.7L | 6.6L |
| **Power (peak)** | 420 hp (HO) / 370 hp (SO) | 475 hp | 445 hp |
| **Torque (peak)** | 1,075 lb-ft (HO) / 850 lb-ft (SO) | 1,050 lb-ft | 910 lb-ft |
| **Torque delivery** | Low RPM — exceptional | Low-to-mid RPM — strong | Mid RPM — smooth |
| **Injection pump** | CP3 (2007.5–2018) / CP4 (SO+HO 2019–2020) / CP3 (2021–2024) / CP8 (2025+) | CP4 (all years) | CP4 (LML 2011–2016) / CP3 (L5P 2017+) |
| **EGR cooler issues** | Moderate | High (2011–2014 especially) | Low |
| **Long-term reputation** | Excellent — Cummins is legendary | Strong after 2015 fixes | Excellent |

**Cummins I6 vs. V8 diesels:** The Cummins inline-6 is inherently simpler to work on — no valley between cylinder banks, better access. The V8 layout (Power Stroke, Duramax) is more compact but denser. Cummins has a decades-long reputation in commercial trucks that the V8 diesels simply don't match in longevity data, though both are strong for normal use.

## Transmission Comparison

| | Ram 3500 | Ford F-350 | Chevy/GMC |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Transmission** | Aisin AS69RC (HO) / 68RFE (SO) | TorqShift 10R140 | Allison 10L1000 |
| **Speeds** | 6 (both) | 10 | 10 |
| **Reputation** | Aisin: excellent. 68RFE: adequate but weaker | Strong — 10-speed handles load well | **Best in class** — Allison is legendary |
| **Aftermarket support** | Good | Good | Excellent |
| **Notes** | Upgrade to Aisin if buying Ram HO | No major issues 2017+ | Allison-built, not GM-built. Different class |

**Allison advantage:** The Allison 10L1000 in Chevy/GMC trucks is not a GM-manufactured transmission — it's built by Allison Transmission, a separate company with decades of commercial transmission experience. The Allison's torque converter and shift logic are calibrated specifically for heavy towing. Owner consensus across forums is that the Allison is the smoothest towing transmission in the class, with fewer complaints and longer rebuild intervals.

## Emissions System Reliability

All four trucks run full emissions equipment: DPF (diesel particulate filter), DEF (diesel exhaust fluid / SCR), and EGR (exhaust gas recirculation). The reliability of these systems varies.

| System | Ram Cummins | Ford Power Stroke | Duramax |
|---|---|---|---|
| **DPF clogging** | Moderate | Moderate | Moderate — all trucks benefit from highway miles |
| **EGR cooler failures** | Occasional | Common (2011–2014) | Rare |
| **DEF system issues** | Occasional — pump, sensor | Occasional | Occasional |
| **Emissions delete** | Illegal — federal law (EPA), significant fines. Do not do this | Same | Same |
| **Regen frequency** | Depends on duty cycle — short trips increase regen | Same | Same |

> **Highway vs. city:** All diesel emissions systems are happier on highway miles than city/short-trip use. A truck used for 15-minute commutes will have more DPF and EGR issues than one used for 200-mile highway pulls. If your use case is predominantly towing and highway travel, emissions issues are less likely.

| **Front locking diff** | Not standard | Not standard | Not standard | Not standard |

**For the use case (dispersed camping with a travel trailer):** The GMC Sierra 3500 AT4 is the standout. It's the only 3500 with a factory off-road package that includes upgraded suspension (Multimatic DSSV dampers), skid plates, and AT4-specific tuning. The Ford Tremor on an F-350 is the second strongest option. Ram and Chevy don't offer a purpose-built 3500 off-road package.

- **Ford F-350**: Strong warranty support, highest tow ratings, Tremor available
- **Chevy/GMC**: AT4 (GMC) is the only 3500 with a factory off-road package; CP3 pump on all; Allison transmission

## Honest Verdict

**For the specific use case** (towing a 10,000–14,000 lb travel trailer, dispersed off-grid camping, long-term retirement ownership):

| Priority | Winner | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Best tow ratings** | Ford F-350 | 40,000 lbs; highest in class |
| **Best engine torque** | Ram 3500 HO | 1,075 lb-ft; best low-RPM pull |
| **Safest injection pump** | Chevy/GMC L5P (2017+) | CP3 on L5P — LML (2011–2016) has CP4, same risk as Ford |
| **Best transmission** | Chevy/GMC | Allison — not even close |
| **Best off-road (3500)** | GMC Sierra AT4 | Only 3500 with factory off-road package |
| **Best used buy (safety)** | Ram 3500 SO (2013–2018) | CP3 pump, legendary Cummins, strong resale |
| **Best all-around new** | GMC Sierra 3500 AT4 | Duramax + Allison + CP3 + off-road package |

> **The community says it plainly:** For pure towing performance, Ram Cummins wins on torque feel. For reliability and worry-free ownership — especially used — the Duramax/Allison combination in Chevy/GMC is the most defensible choice. The CP4 pump risk is real, documented, and expensive when it fails. If you buy a Ford or Ram HO, budget for CP4 mitigation from day one.

## The Recommendation

**Use case:** Towing a travel trailer in retirement. Dispersed off-grid camping on forest roads. Long-term ownership. Needs to run 150,000+ miles with minimal drama.

### First Choice: GMC Sierra 3500 AT4 (2021–2023 used, or new)

This is the truck the research points to for the stated use case. It is the only 1-ton in this comparison that combines all four of the things that matter most:

1. **CP3 injection pump** — no catastrophic fuel system risk, new or used
2. **Allison 10L1000** — best towing transmission in the class
3. **AT4 package** — the only factory off-road option on a 1-ton GM truck; DSSV suspension handles loaded forest roads better than any other 1-ton from the factory
4. **Skid plates + DuraTrac tires** — engineered in, not bolted on aftermarket

The tow rating gap vs. Ford (36K vs. 40K lbs) is irrelevant for a travel trailer in the 10,000–14,000 lb range. You will never use either number. The gap in reliability and ownership peace of mind over a 15–20 year retirement horizon is not irrelevant.

**Target:** 2021–2022 used Sierra 3500 AT4 with L5P Duramax. Run VIN through NHTSA. Confirm Allison TCM software is current. Done.

---

### Budget Alternative: Ram 3500 High Output (2016–2018, 4th Gen)

If the retirement budget needs capital elsewhere right now, the 4th gen Ram 3500 HO is the legitimate second choice. Key insight: **the 4th gen HO (2013–2018) uses CP3, not CP4** — that only changed in 2019. So you can get Cummins + CP3 + Aisin in a 2016–2018 HO.

- **Cummins I6 with CP3** — same "safe pump" status as the L5P Duramax
- **Aisin AS69RC** — bulletproof 6-speed; arguably as durable as the Allison for this use case
- **Significantly cheaper used** than a 2021+ Sierra AT4 — often $15,000–$25,000 less
- **Strong resale** — Cummins reputation drives demand; you won't lose badly if you sell

**Note on SO vs. HO in 4th gen:** Both use CP3. The difference is transmission: SO gets the 68RFE (weaker under sustained tow load), HO gets the Aisin (excellent). If buying a 4th gen Ram for heavy towing, target the HO — you get CP3 AND Aisin. There is no scenario where the SO gets an Aisin; that pairing doesn't exist.

**The trade-off:** No factory off-road package. Add AT tires (~$1,200) and aftermarket skid plates (~$800–$1,200) and you're most of the way there mechanically for a fraction of the price difference.

**Avoid on Ram:** Any 2019–2020 HO without confirmed recall (CP4 recall replaced the pump with CP3 — verify at nhtsa.gov). Any 68RFE truck for heavy sustained towing. Document VGT actuator history on any used Cummins.
