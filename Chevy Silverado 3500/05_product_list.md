# Chevy Silverado 3500 HD — Product List
*Recommended add-ons for towing a travel trailer + dispersed camping use*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> **Priority rating:** 🔴 Essential — do this before towing seriously | 🟠 Highly recommended | 🟡 Nice to have | ⬜ Optional/situational

> **Good news:** The L5P Duramax with CP3 pump and Allison transmission is the lowest-drama used diesel in the segment. The product list here is shorter and cheaper than the Ford equivalent because you're not managing CP4 risk.

---

## Fuel System

### 🟡 Fuel Lubricity Additive *(L5P, 2017+ — nice to have, not mandatory)*

The CP3 pump is far more tolerant of US diesel quality than the CP4. An additive still helps injector cleanliness and fuel system longevity, but it's not the CP4 life-insurance play it is on the Ford.

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Power Service Diesel Kleen (silver bottle) | ~$12–$15/quart | Good all-around additive |
| Stanadyne Performance Formula | ~$10–$14/quart | Cummins OEM-endorsed; works on Duramax too |

**If buying LML (2011–2016) with CP4:** Fuel additive becomes 🔴 Essential. Same rules as the Ford.

---

### ⬜ Upgraded Fuel Filtration *(L5P — optional; LML with CP4 — recommended)*

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| FASS Titanium Series (100 GPH) | ~$550–$650 | Lower flow rate version adequate for Duramax |
| AirDog II-4G (100 GPH) | ~$500–$600 | Good alternative |

For L5P (2017+): Optional — the factory filtration is adequate with CP3. For LML (2011–2016) with CP4: Add this.

## Exhaust Brake

### 🟠 Aftermarket Exhaust Brake *(all Duramax — no factory exhaust brake)*

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| BD Diesel Exhaust Brake (L5P / LML) | ~$600–$900 installed | Best known option for Duramax |
| Banks Power Brake | ~$500–$800 installed | Alternative; works well with Banks gauge system |

**Priority assessment:** The Allison in Tow/Haul mode provides meaningful engine braking — better than the Ford TorqShift baseline. But it's still not a Cummins exhaust brake. For flat highway towing, the Allison is fine. For mountain towing with a heavy trailer, consider the exhaust brake.

## Allison Transmission

### 🟠 Allison TranSynd 668 Fluid *(stock up — hard to find at auto parts stores)*

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Allison TranSynd 668 (1 gallon) | ~$25–$35/gallon | Buy 4+ gallons for a pan drop service |
| Allison TranSynd 668 (case of 12) | ~$250–$350 | Best price per gallon |

Available at Truck stops, Allison dealers, and Amazon. This is not optional — no substitutes.

## Off-Road / Recovery

### 🟠 Skid Plates *(Silverado 3500 has none standard)*

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| SDHQ Chevy 2500/3500 HD Skid Plate System | ~$600–$900 installed | Transfer case, fuel tank, oil pan |
| Rough Country Silverado HD Skid Plates | ~$300–$500 | Budget option; steel protection |

The Silverado 3500 has no factory off-road package and no skid plates. For dispersed camping on forest roads, this is the biggest gap vs. the GMC Sierra AT4.

---

### 🟠 All-Terrain Tires *(if not equipped)*

Most Silverado 3500s come with all-season highway tires from the factory. For dispersed camping on unpaved roads:

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Goodyear Wrangler DuraTrac LT285/70R17 or LT285/75R18 | ~$250–$320 each | Factory tire on GMC AT4; good choice |
| BFGoodrich All-Terrain T/A KO2 | ~$280–$350 each | Premium off-road all-terrain; excellent reputation |
| Falken Wildpeak A/T3W | ~$220–$280 each | Great value AT tire; strong off-road + highway balance |

**Recommendation:** Falken Wildpeak AT3W for value, BFG KO2 for premium. Budget ~$1,000–$1,400 for all four.
