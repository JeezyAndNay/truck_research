# Chevy Silverado 3500 HD — Known Issues by Year
*6.6L Duramax LML (2011–2016) / L5P (2017–Present) · Allison · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> ⚠️ **CRITICAL CORRECTION FROM DEEP DIVE — READ THIS FIRST:**
> The 6.6L Duramax LML (2011–2016) uses the **Bosch CP4.2** injection pump — NOT the CP3.
> The CP3 applies only to the L5P (2017+) and all pre-2011 Duramax engines (LB7, LLY, LBZ, LMM).
> If you are looking at a used Duramax truck from 2011–2016, **it carries the same CP4 risk as the Ford F-350.**
> The "safe CP3" advantage kicks in at 2017 and later only.

## 2011–2014 — LML Duramax, First Years

The LML replaced the LMM in 2011. It was a major emissions update — new SCR/DEF system, EGR changes, and the switch from CP3 to CP4.2.

### 🔴 CP4.2 Injection Pump *(2011–2016 LML)*
- GM switched from CP3 to CP4.2 on the LML to achieve higher injection pressure with the new emissions system
- Same failure mode as Ford's CP4: metal shrapnel contaminates injectors, rails, lines, tank
- **Cost of failure:** $8,000–$20,000 for full fuel system remediation
- The aftermarket responded immediately: CP3 conversion kits are available from WC Fab, Fleece Performance, BD Diesel, S&S Motorsports, and Industrial Injection
- Some conversion kits are 50-state CARB-compliant; verify before purchasing if emissions testing applies
- **Recommendation:** Any LML purchase should either include a CP3 conversion already done, or price that conversion in ($1,200–$2,500 installed)

### 🟠 EGR Cooler Failure *(2011–2014)*
- EGR cooler failures were more frequent on early LML production
- Symptoms: white smoke, coolant loss, elevated temps
- **Fix:** EGR cooler replacement ($400–$900 parts + labor)

### 🟠 DEF System — Early DEF Pump & Doser Failures *(2013–2014)*
- 2013 was the first LML year with SCR/DEF (some 2011–2012s had pre-production DEF depending on market)
- DEF dosing pump failures and DEF injector (doser) crystallization were common
- Codes: P20EE, P203F, P2BAD
- **Fix:** DEF pump replacement ($400–$800). DEF doser replacement ($200–$500).

### 🟠 NOx Sensor Failure *(2013–2016)*
- Common across early DEF-equipped Duramax trucks
- Triggers reduced-power/derate events
- **Fix:** Sensor replacement ($200–$400)

### 🟡 Allison 6-Speed Fluid Maintenance *(all Allison years)*
- The 6-speed Allison 1000 requires Allison-specified TranSynd fluid
- Using wrong fluid accelerates wear and shortens fluid life
- Under heavy towing, change fluid every 25,000 miles (not 50,000+)
- Allison is extremely durable with correct fluid — neglect is the enemy

## 2015–2016 — LML Duramax, Late Production

### 🔴 CP4.2 *(continues through 2016)*
- Still CP4.2 in 2015–2016 LML trucks
- By 2025–2026, these trucks are 9–11 years old and CP4 failures are increasingly reported in the enthusiast community
- **Recommendation:** CP3 conversion is strongly advisable on any 2015–2016 LML purchase

### 🟠 DEF System *(ongoing, more components aging out)*
- DEF tank assembly failures, DEF heater failures, and DEF pump failures increasing as trucks age
- 2015–2016 DEF system is more refined than 2013–2014, but components age
- **Fix:** Component-by-component replacement as needed. DEF tank assembly replacement is the expensive one ($800–$1,500 including labor).

### 🟡 EGR Cooler *(improved but present)*
- Less common than 2011–2014 but still occurs
- **Fix:** Same as earlier years

## 2017 — L5P Duramax Debut ✅ CP3 Returns

2017 was a significant milestone: the new L5P Duramax replaced the LML, and with it, GM returned to the CP3 injection pump. This is the year the Duramax becomes the safest used diesel in the segment.

### ✅ CP3 — Safe from Here Forward
- L5P uses Bosch CP3 — gradual failure mode, no catastrophic contamination
- Replacement cost: ~$1,000–$2,500 installed (vs. $8,000–$20,000 for CP4 failure)

### 🟠 Water Pump Failure *(2017–2018, worst years)*
- L5P water pump failures were concentrated in the first two model years
- Symptoms: coolant leak, overheating
- GM released TSBs; many were repaired under warranty
- **Fix:** Water pump replacement (~$400–$900 installed). Any used 2017–2018 should have records showing this was addressed or wasn't an issue.

### 🟠 Coolant Air Pocket / Bleeding Issue *(early 2017 production)*
- Some early L5P trucks left the factory with air trapped in the cooling system
- Results in overheating events on first hard drives
- **Fix:** Cooling system bleed procedure; proper coolant fill. DIY or dealer — quick fix.

### 🟡 Fuel Injector Imbalance Codes *(2017–present)*
- The L5P high-pressure injection system generates "injector balance" codes (P0263–P0278 range) as a maintenance indicator
- Not a hard failure — indicates an injector is starting to drift from spec
- **Fix:** Monitor and replace injectors as needed. Individual injector replacement ~$400–$600 each installed. Watch for balance codes pulling more than one injector low simultaneously — that may indicate a CP3 issue.

## 2018–2019 — L5P, 3rd Generation Body

### 🟡 Water Pump *(less common than 2017)*
- 2018 still saw some water pump failures but less frequently than 2017
- 2019 largely clean on this issue

### 🟡 DPF Clogging *(2017–2019, short-trip use)*
- L5P DPF needs periodic highway-speed regeneration cycles to burn off accumulated soot
- Trucks used primarily for short trips, towing at low speed, or lots of idle time develop DPF clogging faster
- Symptoms: increased regen frequency, reduced power, check engine
- **Fix:** Forced DPF regen at dealer, or extended highway drive. Keep highway miles in the rotation.

### 🟡 DEF Tank Heater Failure *(2017–2019, cold climates)*
- Still present on L5P-era trucks in cold-weather markets
- **Fix:** Heater element replacement ($300–$600)

---

## 2020–2021 — 4th Generation Body, New Allison 10-Speed

### ✅ Same CP3, same L5P
- No change to fuel system in 4th gen
- CP3 continues; still the safest fuel system in the segment

### 🟠 Allison 10-Speed Shift Programming *(2020–2021)*
- The new 10L1000 launched with shift quality complaints — harsh in some scenarios, inconsistent lockup behavior
- GM released multiple TCM (transmission control module) software updates (TSBs)
- **Recommendation:** For any 2020–2021 purchase, confirm Allison TCM updates are current at dealer
- By 2022+, shift behavior was largely resolved

### 🟡 DEF Tank Heater *(continues)*
- Ongoing across model years in cold climates

### 🟡 DPF Clogging *(ongoing if truck is used short-trip)*
- Same L5P DPF sensitivity as 2017+

### 🟡 Coolant System Maintenance
- 2020+ trucks are newer; less data but cooling system quality checks should happen at 60K+

---

## 2022–2024 — Current Production

### ✅ L5P + CP3 + Allison 10L1000
- The safest drivetrain combination in the 1-ton class for used buying
- No known systemic first-run issues on current production

### 🟡 Allison Fluid — Critical Maintenance
- Allison TranSynd requirement: every 50,000 miles normal use, every 25,000 miles heavy tow
- This is the single highest-value maintenance item on any Duramax/Allison truck

## Summary: Worst Years vs. Best Years

| Category | Worst Years | Best Years |
|---|---|---|
| Injection pump | **2011–2016 LML (CP4.2)** ⚠️ | 2017+ L5P (CP3) ✅ |
| Water pump | 2017–2018 | 2019+ |
| EGR system | 2011–2014 | 2017+ |
| Allison reliability | All years (with correct fluid) | All years |
| DEF system | 2013–2016 | 2020+ |
| Overall | 2011–2014 | 2017–2019 used / 2022+ new |

**Best used buy:** 2017–2019 L5P Silverado 3500 with 6-speed Allison. CP3, strong L5P engine, 6-speed Allison, depreciation absorbed. If budget allows and you want the 10-speed, confirm TSB software is current on 2020–2021.

**Avoid:** Any 2011–2016 LML without a CP3 conversion already installed OR a significant price reduction to account for the conversion cost and risk.
