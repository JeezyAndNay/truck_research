# Chevy Silverado 3500 HD — Pre-Purchase Checklist
*6.6L Duramax L5P · Allison 10L1000 · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Print or use on your phone at the seller's location*

---

> **Buy target: 2017+ L5P only.** The 2011–2016 LML Duramax uses a CP4.2 injection pump — same catastrophic failure risk as the Ford Power Stroke. This checklist assumes you are buying an L5P (2017+). If looking at an LML, treat it like the Ford checklist: CP4 mitigation is mandatory and the mileage window is tighter.

---

## Before You Go — Documentation

- [ ] Confirm model year is **2017 or newer** (L5P Duramax with CP3 pump)
- [ ] Run VIN on [nhtsa.gov/recalls](https://www.nhtsa.gov/vehicle/recalls) — check for open recalls
- [ ] Run VIN on [vpic.nhtsa.dot.gov/decoder](https://vpic.nhtsa.dot.gov/decoder/) — confirm engine (L5P), transmission (Allison 10L1000)
- [ ] Pull Carfax or AutoCheck — accidents, flood, title issues, odometer rollback
- [ ] Request maintenance records — specifically Allison TranSynd 668 fluid change history and oil change intervals
- [ ] Ask for original window sticker or build sheet if available

---

## Allison Transmission — Most Important Maintenance Item

- [ ] Confirm Allison 10L1000 (all L5P Silverado 3500s — not an option, it's standard)
- [ ] Ask for TranSynd 668 fluid change records — **no substitute fluid is acceptable**; if owner used Dexron instead, the transmission has been running on wrong fluid
- [ ] Under tow load interval: every 25,000 miles. Normal driving: 45,000–50,000 miles
- [ ] If no Allison service records and high mileage: price in a fluid + filter service ($250–$400)
- [ ] Check fluid: should be red/pink and clean. Dark/burnt = past-due service

---

## Injection Pump Confirmation

- [ ] Verify 2017+ (L5P) = CP3 pump — no CP4 risk. No further pump action needed
- [ ] If any doubt about year: VIN position 10 = model year. '8' = 2017, '9' = 2018, 'K' = 2019, 'L' = 2020, 'M' = 2021, 'N' = 2022, 'P' = 2023, 'R' = 2024
- [ ] **If looking at 2011–2016 LML:** stop here and use the Ford checklist logic for CP4 — different pump, same failure risk

---

## Exterior

- [ ] Panel gaps consistent — uneven gaps suggest prior accident repair
- [ ] All four wheel arches: check for rust bubbling under paint
- [ ] Rocker panels: check for rust or damage
- [ ] Bed: check corners, floor, and tailgate for rust; check under bedliner if equipped
- [ ] Hitch receiver: no cracks at weld points, no severe rust inside tube
- [ ] 7-pin trailer plug: inspect pins, no bent/corroded contacts
- [ ] Tires: adequate tread depth (>4/32"), even wear pattern, DOT date code under 6 years
- [ ] All lights functional: headlights, brake, reverse, turn signals, marker lights

---

## Under Hood

- [ ] **Engine oil:** pull dipstick — should be amber/light brown (0W-20 dexos D). Milky/foamy = coolant contamination (serious)
- [ ] **Oil capacity is 8 quarts** — much less than Cummins; don't confuse oil level with an overfill if switching from a Ram
- [ ] **Coolant:** check reservoir level and color — DexCool (orange). Milky = head gasket concern
- [ ] No visible coolant leaks around EGR cooler connections or hoses
- [ ] **Water pump (2017–2018 L5P specifically):** look for weeping or staining around the water pump snout. This is a known early L5P issue — ask if it's been replaced
- [ ] Fuel filter housing: no leaks or weeping
- [ ] Battery terminals: clean and tight
- [ ] Turbo: no oil weeping around seals; shaft play within normal range

---

## Under the Truck

- [ ] Frame rails: no visible cracks, weld repairs, or heavy rust
- [ ] Transfer case: no leaks, check drain plug area for fresh oil
- [ ] Front driveshaft and U-joints: check for play/wear
- [ ] Front differential: no leaks
- [ ] Rear differential: no leaks at pinion seal or cover
- [ ] **G80 auto-locker:** confirm equipped if expected (not standard on all trims). Test: drive slowly in a tight circle on dirt/gravel and listen for the locker engagement click
- [ ] Exhaust: no rust-through or cracks, DPF intact
- [ ] DEF tank: no leaks, check DEF level
- [ ] **No factory skid plates** on Silverado 3500 — note if aftermarket plates are installed

---

## Interior & Electronics

- [ ] Connect OBD scanner — check for stored DTCs, particularly transmission or DEF codes
- [ ] No warning lights after engine warmup
- [ ] DEF gauge reads correctly, no DEF fault codes
- [ ] **DEF gauge software update (2020–2021):** ask if dealer TSB has been applied — some trucks showed false low DEF warnings
- [ ] Air conditioning: blows cold
- [ ] Infotainment and backup camera: functional
- [ ] 4WD selector: test 4H and 4L engagement
- [ ] Seats, door seals: check for water intrusion staining on headliner and carpet

---

## Test Drive — Minimum 20 Minutes, Mixed Speeds

- [ ] **Cold start:** clean start, no excessive white smoke after 2 minutes
- [ ] **Allison shifts:** should be smooth and confident. Hunting between gears, harsh banging, or shuddering = fluid or calibration issue
- [ ] **Tow/Haul mode:** engage and confirm Allison holds lower gears under deceleration — provides meaningful engine braking
- [ ] **No factory exhaust brake** — Allison Tow/Haul compensates. For mountain towing, note that aftermarket exhaust brake may be advisable
- [ ] **Turbo:** smooth spool, no unusual whistling or surging
- [ ] **Acceleration:** clean, linear. Hesitation or stumble can indicate injector issues
- [ ] **Highway cruise:** no vibrations, no wander, tracks straight
- [ ] **Braking:** firm pedal, no pulling, no pulsating
- [ ] **4WD:** 4H and 4L — no grinding, no clunking
- [ ] **No new warning lights** after 15+ minutes of driving

---

## Truck-Specific Watch Items by Mileage

| Mileage | Items to Scrutinize |
|---|---|
| Any | Allison fluid type and change history — most important item |
| 2017–2018 trucks | Water pump history — ask if it's been replaced |
| 100K+ | EGR cooler inspection; injector balance rates via scanner |
| 150K+ | Allison fluid condition check; full pre-purchase inspection recommended |
| 200K+ | Pre-purchase inspection mandatory; budget for injectors if not recently done |

---

## Mileage Assessment

- [ ] **Under 150K** — confident buy on L5P with documented Allison service
- [ ] **150K–200K** — acceptable with Allison TranSynd 668 records and oil change history
- [ ] **200K+** — pre-purchase diesel shop inspection mandatory
- [ ] **2017–2018 under 150K:** ask specifically about water pump replacement

---

## Pre-Purchase Inspection (any truck over 80K miles)

- [ ] Booked at a diesel-specialist shop or GM dealer familiar with Duramax ($150–$200)
- [ ] Requested: compression test, scanner live data, Allison health check, physical inspection
- [ ] Injector balance rates within spec
- [ ] Allison fluid condition confirmed
- [ ] No active or pending fault codes

---

## Final Decision Gate

- [ ] Model year confirmed 2017+ (L5P, CP3 pump) ✓
- [ ] Allison TranSynd 668 service history confirmed or acceptable ✓
- [ ] Transmission shifts smooth, no hunting or shudder ✓
- [ ] No active DTCs ✓
- [ ] Water pump history reviewed (2017–2018 trucks) ✓
- [ ] Mileage within acceptable range for documentation level ✓
- [ ] Pre-purchase inspection passed (if applicable) ✓
- [ ] Price negotiated to reflect condition ✓

> **All boxes checked? You're ready to buy.**
> **Any unchecked? Negotiate the item into the price or walk.**
