# Chevy Silverado 3500 HD — Maintenance Schedule
*6.6L Duramax L5P V8 · Allison 10L1000 · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> **Towing context:** All intervals below assume regular towing use (10,000–14,000 lb trailer, mixed highway/dispersed camping). Under tow load = severe duty. Use the shorter intervals below.

> **LML note (2011–2016):** If you're running an LML Duramax, it has the CP4.2 pump. Fuel filter maintenance is critical — same as the Ford notes. 2017+ L5P owners have CP3 and less anxiety, but fuel filter maintenance still matters.

---

## Engine Oil

| Item | Spec | Interval (towing) | Interval (normal) | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Engine oil | 0W-20 dexos D (L5P) or 5W-40 | **5,000–7,500 mi** | 7,500 mi | ~8 qts w/ filter |

**Recommended oils:**
- ACDelco dexos D 0W-20 (OEM for L5P, 2017+)
- Shell Rotella T6 5W-40 (compatible; many owners prefer for towing)
- Mobil Delvac 1 ESP 5W-40

**Notes:**
- The L5P specifies dexos D (diesel-specific dexos) — not to be confused with dexos1 (gas engines)
- 8 quarts is the correct L5P capacity — much lower than the Cummins (12 qts) — don't overfill
- Factory Oil Life Monitor tracks multiple inputs; under towing it will typically alert closer to 5,000–7,500 miles
- Always change the filter with the oil

---

## Fuel Filters

| Item | Interval (towing) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fuel filter | **15,000 mi** | Single filter system on L5P |

**Notes:**
- The L5P has a single spin-on fuel filter (simpler than the Cummins dual system)
- Use AC Delco or Duramax-specific filter — maintains correct micron rating
- L5P with CP3: less urgency than CP4 trucks, but don't skip intervals
- **LML (2011–2016) with CP4:** Treat fuel filter maintenance the same as the Ford — 10,000–15,000 mi max, add lubricity additive
- Drain any water from the separator at each filter change

## Allison Transmission Fluid

### 10L1000 (2020+)
| Item | Spec | Interval (towing) | Interval (normal) | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Trans fluid | **Allison TranSynd 668** | **25,000 mi** | 45,000–50,000 mi | ~16 qts |

### Allison 1000 6-Speed (2001–2019)
| Item | Spec | Interval (towing) | Interval (normal) | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Trans fluid | **Allison TranSynd 668** | **25,000 mi** | 45,000–50,000 mi | ~14 qts |

**Notes:**
- **TranSynd is non-negotiable.** Allison specifically engineered these transmissions for TranSynd fluid. Substituting with Dexron or "Allison-compatible" fluids from third parties voids the Allison warranty and shortens transmission life.
- Available at most truck stops, Allison dealers, and online
- Pan drop + filter change — not a flush
- The Allison is extremely durable with correct fluid. Neglect is the primary failure mode.

## Rear Differential

| Item | Spec | Interval | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rear diff fluid | 75W-90 synthetic gear oil (add friction modifier if G80 equipped) | 30,000 mi (towing) / 60,000 mi (normal) | ~3.5 qts |

**Notes:**
- If G80 automatic locking differential: use gear oil with a limited-slip friction modifier additive
- GM recommends specific additive — check owner's manual

## Coolant

| Item | Spec | Interval | Capacity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Engine coolant | Dexcool (OAT) — GM spec | Every 5 years or 150,000 mi | ~6 gallons |

- Dexcool only — do not mix with green coolant
- Check condition annually; if orange coolant has turned brown, flush immediately

| DEF tank heater (cold climates) | Inspect annually | Failure causes limp mode in freezing temps |
| DEF pump | Replace when failed | ~$400–$800 |

## Water Pump (2017–2018 L5P Specific)

| Item | Interval | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Water pump inspection | At first sign of leak or 100,000 mi | Early L5P (2017–2018) had higher water pump failure rate |

If buying a used 2017–2018 L5P, ask if the water pump has been replaced. If not, budget for it.

## Brake Service

| Item | Interval | Notes |

| Front brake pads | Inspect at 25,000 mi | |
| Rear brake pads | Inspect at 30,000 mi | |

**Notes:**
- No factory exhaust brake on Duramax — the Allison provides meaningful engine braking in Tow/Haul but it's not equivalent to a Cummins exhaust brake
- Aftermarket exhaust brake available (BD Diesel) for mountain towing

---

## Annual Inspection Checklist

- [ ] All fluid levels
- [ ] Allison fluid condition check (should be red/pink, not dark)
- [ ] Fuel filter water separator drain
- [ ] DEF system test (especially heater in cold climates)
- [ ] EGR cooler inspection
- [ ] Coolant condition check
- [ ] Trailer hitch and receiver inspection
- [ ] Brake controller calibration
- [ ] DPF condition (forced regen if needed)
- [ ] Air filter condition
