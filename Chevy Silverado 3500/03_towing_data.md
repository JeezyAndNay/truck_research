# Chevy Silverado 3500 HD — Towing Data
*6.6L Duramax L5P V8 · Allison 10L1000 · SRW · Crew Cab · 4x4*
*Research date: June 2026*

---

> **How to read towing charts:** Published max ratings are always for the most favorable configuration under ideal conditions — correct axle ratio, max tow package, right transmission. Your actual truck may rate lower. The door jamb sticker on your specific truck is the only number that matters for your rig.

## What Actually Determines Your Rating

### Axle Ratio
- **3.73 axle:** Standard with Duramax and Max Trailering Package. Unlocks the 36,000 lb max rating.
- **3.42 axle:** Base configuration on lower trims. Significantly lower tow capacity.
- **Recommendation:** Confirm the truck has a 3.73 axle and Max Trailering Package. Check build sheet or door jamb.

### Max Trailering Package
Required for max ratings on Silverado 3500. Includes:
- 3.73 rear axle
- Heavy-duty trailer brake controller
- Upgraded cooling
- Class V 2.5" receiver hitch
- Trailer-specific wiring upgrades

### Payload (SRW, Crew Cab, 4x4, Duramax)
- **Max possible:** ~7,442 lbs (specific lightweight configurations)
- **Typical retail crew cab:** ~3,500–4,500 lbs depending on trim and options
- **Higher trim levels** (LTZ, High Country) tend toward the lower end due to added feature weight

> Payload varies significantly by build. Always check the door jamb sticker for your specific truck.

## What Matters for Your Use Case

**Travel trailer at 10,000–14,000 lbs:**

| Metric | Your Trailer | Silverado 3500 | Headroom |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tow rating | 10,000–14,000 lbs | 36,000 lbs | Substantial |
| Payload (tongue + cargo) | ~2,500–3,000 lbs est. | 3,500–4,500 lbs typical | Adequate |
| GCWR | ~21,000–22,000 lbs loaded | ~40,000+ lbs | Well within |

For a travel trailer in the 10,000–14,000 lb range, the Silverado 3500 is substantially overspecced. The payload, not the tow rating, is what to watch.

## Hitch Receiver

- **Class V 2.5" receiver** standard with Max Trailering Package
- **Integrated brake controller:** Standard on LT and above
- **Tow/Haul mode:** Enables auto-grade braking, sharpens Allison shift logic under load, activates descent control. Use it — it's well-calibrated.

---

## G80 Locker and Towing

The optional G80 Eaton locking rear differential engages automatically when wheel slip is detected. Useful at:
- Wet boat ramps
- Soft ground campsite entry/exit
- Icy grades when launching with load

The G80 does not affect towing ratings. It disengages above ~25 mph automatically.

## Real-World Towing Notes

- **Allison shift behavior:** The Allison 10L1000 in Tow/Haul mode is widely regarded as the smoothest and most confident towing transmission in the class. Holds gears on grades, manages descents with engine braking, shifts seamlessly. Owner consensus: it "feels" heavier and more confident under load than the Ram or Ford transmissions.
- **No factory exhaust brake (Duramax):** Unlike Cummins, the Duramax does not have a dedicated exhaust brake. The Allison's transmission braking and Tow/Haul mode provide meaningful engine braking, but it's not the same as a Cummins exhaust brake. BD Diesel makes an aftermarket exhaust brake for the L5P.
- **DPF regen under tow:** Regeneration is less frequent at sustained highway speeds. Short-trip city driving before a tow trip can trigger a regen mid-pull — not dangerous, but annoying. Run the truck on the highway before hitching up.
- **Fuel economy towing:** Expect 9–13 MPG depending on trailer, speed, and grade.

---

## Comparison: Silverado vs Sierra Towing

The Silverado 3500 and Sierra 3500 HD have identical tow ratings in all configurations — same engine, same transmission, same axles. The only difference that affects towing on the Sierra is the AT4 package:

- AT4's ~2" lift raises the hitch point. May require a drop ball mount depending on trailer coupler height.
- DSSV suspension absorbs road imperfections better under tow load vs. standard shocks.
- No change to published tow ratings.
