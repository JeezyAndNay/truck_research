# Truck Research — Jeezy & Renay

Independent buyer's research for a 1-ton diesel tow vehicle. Built from owner forums, reliability data, and real-world towing numbers — not manufacturer marketing.

**Repo:** https://github.com/JeezyAndNay/truck_research
**Research started:** June 2026
**Goal:** Identify the best 1-ton SRW crew cab 4x4 diesel for towing a travel trailer in retirement, with strong off-road capability and reasonable long-term ownership costs.

---

## Trucks Under Evaluation

| Truck | Engine | Transmission | Max Tow (SRW) | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ram 3500** | 6.7L Cummins I6 | Aisin AS69RC / 68RFE | 37,090 lbs | Best torque in class; CP4 pump risk on 2019+ HO |
| **Ford F-350** | 6.7L Power Stroke V8 | TorqShift 10R140 | 40,000 lbs | Highest tow rating; CP4 risk on 2011–2016 |
| **Chevy Silverado 3500** | 6.6L Duramax L5P | Allison 10-speed | 36,000 lbs | Most reliable transmission; CP3 pump standard |
| **GMC Sierra 3500** | 6.6L Duramax L5P | Allison 10-speed | 36,000 lbs | Same platform as Silverado; AT4 adds off-road package |

---

## The Honest Bottom Line

> All four trucks are capable. The differences that actually matter for long-term ownership are the injection pump type (CP3 vs CP4), transmission reputation, and how each manufacturer has handled emissions system reliability. The CP4 pump failure risk on Ford (2011–2016) and Ram (2019+ HO Cummins) is the single most important factor in used truck buying in this class.
